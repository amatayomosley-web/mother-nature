# Run 01 — Hunter, Spring Forest, Hiked-In

Date of run: 2026-05-09
Status: Day 19 in-game, character died (cougar attack, drainage 3)
Run author: William + Claude (design exercise)

## Setup

- **Character**: "Ren" (placeholder name) — Hunter node
- **Biome**: Forest, mid-spring
- **Starting condition**: Hiked in deliberately for a 10-day expedition; four hours from trailhead
- **Weather at start**: Cool overcast, ~7°C, light wind from west; snowmelt complete in low ground, patchy on north slopes
- **Time at start**: Late morning, ~10:30

### Starting kit

Steel knife, small hand-axe, flint-and-steel, canteen 1L, 10m cordage, 6 wire snares, wool blanket, oiled tarp 2×3m, backpack, 3 days preserved food, layered wool clothing, leather boots, brimmed hat. **Bow + 8 arrows** (added retroactively per §15 Hunter starting kit).

### Compendium grants at start

- Expert: deer, rabbit, bear, wolf, fox, grouse, common tracks, field-dressing, basic predator behavior
- Practiced: edible spring plants (fiddleheads, wild onion, dandelion), basic weather signs, hide-curing
- Novice: medicinal plants, distant predator calls, bow handling
- Glimpsed: rarer fauna, deep ecology

### Skill node

Hunter — tracking, predator-reading, field-dressing, butchery, stalking

### Goal

Survive a 10-day expedition. No predetermined end-state.

## Day 1

### Beat 01 — 10:30, choosing camp

Ren stops at a low ridge where a creek bends below. Across the creek a meadow opens; behind, mixed pine/birch climbs higher. Wind from west. She wants a base for 10 days: water access, weather-defensible, downwind of likely game trails so her scent doesn't betray her, dry to sleep on, near firewood.

Reads wind, drainage, and game sign. Expert tier + Hunter skill surface a deer trail NE-SW along the creek and a rabbit warren under a deadfall 30m south. Wind from west means scent carries east — she picks a flat dry shelf 15m up the slope, downwind of the deer trail, ~50m back from the creek.

**Systems invoked**: ambient wind cue · Tracks (§13.3) · perception ladder (§13) · scent-on-grid (§12.8)

**Gaps surfaced**: G3 terrain readability, G4 game-trail rendering, G5 wind direction visualization

### Beat 02 — 10:50, rough camp

Strings tarp between two pine trunks, low against west wind. Bough bed cut from young spruce. Hangs food pack ~30m downwind of sleep area, high branch — bear-conscious storage; no UI prompt, just Expert-tier muscle memory.

**Gaps**: G6 shelter construction, G7 food cache as scent-emitter, G8 cross-domain micro-skills

### Beat 03 — 11:30, scouting + bear encounter

Brings knife, snares, canteen, axe. Walks downwind so her scent trails behind. Stops at every track. Expert tier reads: 4-day-old bear (large, descended into the creek, dug for roots), 12-hour-old deer (doe + two yearlings), fresh rabbit (<2 hours). She also notes NO wolf sign in this drainage.

**11:50 — encounter.** Active focus mode is on cooldown. Passive L2 mutter fires: *"gut sense — something watching."* She freezes. Across the meadow, 80m off, a black bear (mid-size female) browses fresh shoots. Hasn't seen her — she's downwind and against a tree.

She backs the way she came, eyes on the bear, stops every 10 steps. Wind holds. Bear keeps browsing. She logs: "*black bear, female, browsing this meadow, mid-morning.*"

**Gaps**: G9 movement modes, G10 absence tracking

### Beat 04 — 13:00, back at camp

Three of six snares set on the rabbit warren return route. Sits, drinks, eats a strip of smoked meat. Plans afternoon: build proper fire ring (wet wood from snowmelt — §13.4 fires immediately), continue scouting, check snares before dusk.

## Day 1 continued — afternoon, hunt collision, night-1

### Beat 05 — 13:00–16:30, scout extension + snare line

Snare line walked stalking-pace. Active skill: stalking. Passive scent-suppression and footstep-noise reduction firing automatically — neither show on screen, both felt by the *absence* of obvious-novice noise.

She finds: a small spring 600m up; old beaver workings; a fox den; fresh black bear scat ~200m off the female bear's last position.

Sets two more snares — at deer crossing and spring approach. 5 of 6 snares deployed. **All snare actions are post-mastery** for Ren. Net XP gain: near-zero per §14.4 mastery-diminishing-returns. But the spring is a NEW context. Per the novelty multiplier, the *trickle* fires: small XP toward water-source-reading.

Returns camp 16:30. Wood-gathering en route. Picks dry deadfall (passive: trained-fuel-recognition removed).

### Beat 06 — 16:30–17:30, the hunt collision

Cresting a rise, she sees 60m down a meadow slope: a young white-tailed deer. Browsing alone. Wind from her face — deer is downwind. Deer hasn't seen her.

**Decision tree fires hard.** With knife only (no bow yet at this point in original beat), 60m to close on a deer is implausible. She tracks at distance for 20 minutes, confirms it's heading toward the spring, returns to camp to prep an evening waterhole ambush.

**This is the §12.3 combat/weapons gap, hit at full force in original Run 01.** Resolved later in §15 with bow + 8 arrows in Hunter starting kit.

### Beat 07 — 17:45, snare check yields a rabbit

Walking back to camp, Ren detours to the rabbit-warren snare line. **One snare has caught**: a young rabbit, dead about 30 minutes (still warm, no rigor). The noise of the dying rabbit attracted a fox — fresh fox tracks circle the snare site. Ren got lucky; if she'd checked an hour later, the fox would've claimed it.

**Field-dressing**: Ren's Expert; rabbit takes ~10 minutes, **quality grade A** output. ~600 calories of fresh meat, one usable pelt.

### Beat 08 — 18:00–19:30, fire, ambush prep

Builds fire. Quality A. Cooks half the rabbit, packs the rest. Departs 19:30 for the spring ambush.

**Gaps**: G32 fire's three broadcast channels, G33 twilight perception modulation

### Beat 09 — 20:00–22:30, the ambush, night-1 system

Sets up at spring downwind under brush, ~30m from where she expects deer to drink. Patient wait, ~90 in-game minutes. Body cold-tax begins to creep up.

**The deer never comes.** At 21:30 she gives up. Real ambush hunting is mostly *not* getting the kill. **This is the genre commitment to honesty rather than success.**

Walks back in full dark. Burns most of her focus-uses navigating around fallen logs. Arrives camp 22:30, banks the fire to embers, coils into the wool blanket under the tarp. Knife within reach (Camp Stalker §13.2 awareness).

**Gaps**: G34 patience-as-active-skill, G35 "no kill" outcome handling

### Beat 10 — 22:30–05:30, sleep, night-1

Sleeps. Time-skip with conditional wake-triggers.

At 02:00 a wake trigger fires: distant wolf calls, ~800m off. Not threat-range, but the audio rouses her briefly. Listens for a minute, confirms direction (away from camp), goes back to sleep. **Compendium auto-logs**: "wolves, this drainage, night, ~800m southwest."

At 04:00 the wind shifts — west wind dies, light easterly stirs. Now her camp scent drifts the OTHER way. **§13 wind-shift collision (C4) fires for real.** She sleeps through it.

At 05:30 she wakes naturally. **Sleep quality grade B**: ~75% body-meter recovery, slight stiffness, mild cold residual.

**Gaps**: G36 sleep quality equation, G37 fire-decay overnight, G38 auto-compendium-log threshold

## Day 2 — first kill, the bear of the meadow

### Beat 11 — 05:30–07:00, dawn camp

Cold and stiff. Tarp dripping condensation. Fire dead since ~02:00. Body-meter state: warmth -25%, hunger -40%, thirst -55% (canteen drained), tiredness recovered to ~75%.

Revives fire with banked embers (still warm under ash). Eats cold-cooked half-rabbit (~300 cal). Drinks last sip from canteen.

Plan: refill canteen at the spring; walk the 5 deployed snares; possibly attempt dawn ambush there since deer often re-water at first light.

She fills canteen at spring at 06:30. Hunter's Practiced water-reading flagged this source as "low-risk, drink direct."

### Beat 12 — 07:00–09:00, snare line + the dawn ambush

Snare line: 1 catch (small grouse, ~400 cal); 1 sprung-but-empty (fox investigated, didn't get caught); 3 empty. Patches one bent snare. Resets 3.

**At 07:30**, sets up ambush at spring with bow. Wind shifted overnight to NE — she circles 50m to the south side of the spring, settles into brush downwind. **Active skill: stalking** + **passive: scent-suppression**.

**08:15 — the deer comes back.** Same yearling buck. Approaches the spring from east-southeast, wind in its face. Deer drops head to drink — broadside shot, ~25m. Ideal range for a bow.

**The shot.** Ren draws (passive: trained-draw-strength = full draw), aims for the chest cavity behind the shoulder (vital zone). At the moment of release, the deer **lifts its head** — ear twitched at a fox's distant yip. The arrow strikes ~6 inches back of where she aimed: **gut hit**, not vital.

**§15 lethality model fires — wound, not kill.** Deer crashes off through brush, blood trail starting.

**Gap**: G39 animal-movement-at-shot-release (HIGH — needed for §15 to not produce 100%-reliable Expert kills)

### Beat 13 — 08:30–11:30, the recovery

**The decision:** pursue immediately, wait an hour to let the deer bed, or abandon? Ethical hunter rule: wounded deer not pursued will often bed within 200m, weaken, die quietly. Pursued deer runs in adrenaline, can travel kilometers. But weather forecasts late-morning rain (§13.3 weather erasure of blood trail looms).

Ren waits 45 min, then begins pursuit. Hunter's Expert tracking + clear blood-spatter make the trail readable: gut-shot, dragging slightly, weakening, traveling NE.

Trail leads ~600m NE. By 10:00, blood spatter intensifies (deer bedded, wound bleeding more in stillness). Trail enters small thicket of young spruce.

She approaches downwind, slow. Finds the deer bedded, weak but conscious. Mercy shot at 8m: arrow #2 to the head, instant kill.

**Two arrows used. One recovered cleanly from the gut wound. One snapped from the head shot** (force broke shaft against bone). Net loss: one arrow.

**Gap**: G40 arrow recovery and durability per hit context

### Beat 14 — 11:30–14:30, processing + the bear

The deer is ~80kg field-dressed weight. Far from camp (~1.2 km). Ren can't carry the whole animal. Decides: field-dress on site, take the loins + backstraps + heart + liver (highest cal-per-weight), hang the rest 4m up a tree (bear-conscious cache height; bears can climb — risk).

**Field-dressing**: 25 minutes. Expert post-mastery. **Quality grade A**: full yield, clean cut, intact hide saved. ~12kg of prime meat to carry; ~30kg cached.

**12:30 — the bear arrives.**

Ren is repacking when she hears it. **The female bear from yesterday**, drawn by the scent. Approaching from the meadow direction, ~150m off, moving directly toward the kill site.

**§13.2 Carrion Chains in action.** A kill site downwind of an apex individual = inevitable arrival. Ren has minutes.

**Decision tree (real, in fiction time):**
- Run with the meat she has, abandon the cache → keeps 12kg, bear gets 30kg + offal
- Try to defend the cache → bow vs bear is bad odds; she'd likely die
- Retrieve the cache and run → physically impossible in time
- Negotiate → drag offal away, bribe with heart/liver

She runs with the 12kg. Bear takes the cache + remaining carcass.

**Compendium auto-log:**
- "Bear claim: lost 30kg cache + hide + remaining cuts to female bear, this drainage"
- Apex individual identity (G25): the bear is now logged as "the bear of the meadow" — she'll recognize it on future encounters
- §13.2 Carrion Chains progression: bear will hold this site for several days

**§14 mastery-bonuses NOT firing**: even a master hunter wouldn't change this calculus; the bear is too lethal at close range. The decision is *strategic*, not skill-mediated.

**§1 No Dominant Strategy**: a Hunter with a hunting rifle could have defended the cache. But the rifle would have advertised position drainage-wide, possibly attracting other players or wolves. Different weapons, different trade-offs.

**Gaps**: G41 food cache as defensible object, G42 apex predator economic intelligence

### Beat 15 — 14:30–19:00, return + processing + day-2 settling

Long walk back with 12kg pack, mood mixed. Reaches camp ~16:00.

Processes the meat: smokes ~6kg (preservation), cuts ~3kg into strips for tonight + tomorrow's pack, eats heart raw. Saves the hide for later tanning.

Late afternoon: more wolf calls, ~600m east. The Carrion Chains on her abandoned cache might be drawing wolves into the drainage.

18:00 hot meal. **Morale uptick** (full belly, hot food, successful day even with the bear loss).

Sets snares 4 and 5 (newly available) on a fresh route — different locations from yesterday.

19:00 sleeps. Day-2 wraps.

## Days 3-5 — drainage decision, migration

### Beat 16 — Day 3, the drainage decision

The bear of the meadow now holds the deer-kill site as territory. Wolf calls last night were closer — Carrion Chains pulling in larger predators. Ren's drainage is becoming dangerous.

She chooses hybrid approach: spend Day 3 prepping a move, leave at dawn day-4. Snare-checks again (one more rabbit catch).

**Needs surfaced:**
- N1 — Hide processing (deer hide will rot in days untanned)
- N2 — Cooking implement (no pot; birchbark improvisation possible)
- N3 — Salt source (smoke preserves ~1 week; salt needed for longer)

### Beat 17 — Day 4, the move

Ren leaves at first light. Pack: ~28kg. Walks 9 hours through forest, NE direction. Crosses three streams. Climbs a low ridge into a parallel valley. Loses the meadow-drainage from line of sight.

Mid-day encounters: fresh moose tracks (large male), fiddlehead patch (handful, ~50 cal), old human-camp ruins (decades old, decades-old fire ring, tree carved with initials — multiplayer-readable trace).

Settles new camp at dusk under a rock overhang on a south-facing slope, ~50m from a small creek.

**Needs surfaced:**
- N4 — Carry capacity vs travel range
- N5 — Improvised shelter without good tarp anchors
- N6 — Trail-marking system (no minimap; she can't easily find her way back if needed)

### Beat 18 — Day 5, exploring drainage 2

Ren scouts 2km in three directions. Finds: a small creek flows into a pond (fish visible — small trout); bear sign (different bear; "bear, drainage 2, sign only"); patch of wild onions and late-season berries; old beaver-felled tree (dried wood — fuel jackpot); no deer sign yet.

Sets snares (first time in this drainage, novel context, full XP if anything catches). Returns to camp at dusk.

Evening hot meal: smoked deer + boiled wild onion in a *birchbark pot* (improvised cooking). Birchbark + careful low fire = boiled water without burning the bark. Inefficient but works.

**Needs surfaced:**
- N7 — Fishing system
- N8 — Improvised cookware
- N9 — Per-drainage resource profiles

## Days 6-12 — fishing, tanning, storm, wolves, second migration

### Beat 19 — Day 6, hide preservation + first fish

Hide-drying rack from saplings. Hide hung; fire kept alive at moderate output 6+ hours. **Smoke-dry** preservation chosen (Practiced grade B, lasts ~4 weeks).

Fishing: walks 800m to pond. Improvises tackle — cordage line, carved bone hook from yesterday's deer leg, grub bait dug from beaver-felled log. Sits at pond edge for 90 minutes, casting. **One small trout caught** on third attempt. ~250 cal.

**Needs**: N10 bone-hook crafting, N11 long-passive-task time accelerator

### Beat 20 — Day 7, the storm

Mid-morning the wind shifts to NW. Front approaching. Reads sky (passive: weather-recognition partial). ~6 hours warning.

Storm prep, ~3 hours: reinforces tarp anchors, builds windbreak from scavenged deadfall, stocks dry fuel under shelter, brings drying hide inside, caches food cleanly hung in tree.

Storm hits ~14:00. Heavy rain, wind 50 km/h, temperature drops. Lasts ~18 hours. Ren stays in shelter. Tends fire with effort — wet wind makes flame fragile (§13.4 Wet Wood). Twice the fire nearly dies and she relights. Hide gets re-soaked despite shelter.

**Needs**: N12 storm prep micro-tasks, N13 tarp/equipment damage and repair, N14 indoor-vs-outdoor activity zones

### Beat 21 — Day 8-9, recovery + first snare hits

Day 8 recovery. Patches the tarp (~30 min, post-mastery). Re-builds drying rack. Restarts smoke-dry. Walks the snare line carefully.

Snare results: 2 of 4 hit — both rabbits, one fresh, one older (partly fox-damaged). 1 snare missing (washed out by storm runoff). 1 empty.

Day 9: routine maintenance. Forage circuit. Sets fish line at pond overnight.

**Needs**: N15 pelt combining for usable items, N16 overnight passive fishing line spec

### Beat 22 — Day 10, the wolves

Late afternoon, returning from pond with one trout (overnight catch, ~300 cal), Ren crosses an open meadow toward camp. **Wind from her face.** A small wolf pack — 3 individuals — emerges from treeline 80m to her right. They've been ranging east-to-west. They see her.

**Pack utility AI selects: investigate.** They advance at slow trot, fanning slightly to flank.

She has seconds to choose. Drops the fish + brandishes the bow + backs away talking calmly. **Wolves' utility AI re-evaluates**: meat received without fight + threat-display + they're not at attack-readiness yet. They feed on the fish, watch her retreat to ~150m, then resume their travel west. No attack.

**Compendium logs**: "*wolves, drainage 2, pack of 3, mid-sized, accepted offering and disengaged at human display.*"

**Needs**: N17 wolf pack AI, N18 threat-display mechanic, N19 "offering" interaction

### Beat 23 — Day 11-12, migration question

Day 11: Ren weighs whether to stay in drainage 2.

Evidence: deer thin; wolf pack confirmed; bear sign close; fish renewable but limited. Drainage 1 fully predator-claimed. **Decision**: prep a longer migration toward higher elevation foothills.

Day 12 dawn departure: hikes ~14 km north and slightly uphill. Crosses into a higher-elevation valley, mixed conifer + birch with patches of alpine meadow. **Drainage 3.**

**Needs**: N20 multi-day cached storage at abandoned camp, N21 long-distance travel mechanics, N22 elevation as terrain attribute

## Days 13-19 — drainage 3, the cougar arc

### Beat 24 — Days 13-15, settling drainage 3

Cold mornings (frost on grass at this elevation). Body grade-C sleep day 13. Routine across days 13-15: scout, set snares, modest hare catch (1), unsuccessful caribou stalk.

Notes during days 13-14: **Cougar tracks** — small, paw-pad rounded, claws not registered. Expert tier IDs species; what it doesn't fully read is that the same cougar tracks are in *multiple* spots she's walked. She marks the species presence; doesn't yet read it as territorial overlap.

Body trending: tiredness +20% (cumulative), morale slightly down, hunger marginal.

### Beat 25 — Day 16-17, the alpine storm

Day 16 mid-day: front building from west, faster than the drainage-2 storm. Higher altitude = colder. ~6 hours warning.

Storm prep: reinforces tarp, builds windbreak, stocks dry fuel, brings hide inside.

**Storm hits 18:30, lasts ~36 hours.** Wet snow falls 4cm overnight. Wind 70 km/h gusts. Temperature -3°C. Tarp holds. Fire barely sustainable. Ren sleeps poorly (grade-C). Morning of Day 17: stiff hands, slight cough.

By Day 18 dawn: clouds breaking, ground wet snow, fresh tracks everywhere — including more cougar sign, this time within 200m of her camp.

**Compendium observation**: "*cougar sign close to camp, post-storm; tracks show passage during storm hours.*" She reads it as: "a cougar passed through during the storm, possibly using my fire-light to locate."

Body state end of Day 17: warmth 70%, hunger 50%, tiredness 50%, **mild respiratory cough developing**, morale low.

### Beat 26 — Day 18-19, the pressure builds

**Day 18.** Ren needs a kill. Food carried is now 1.5 days. She tracks caribou aggressively — 3 caribou + 1 calf, traveling overnight from storm shelter. Tracks fresh-since-dawn.

**She crosses cougar tracks again.** Same individual based on size. Track is ~6 hours old. Heading the same direction she's heading.

She marks it. Continues hunt — caribou is the priority. **She reads "cougar going same direction as me" as: cougar is hunting the caribou, like I am. Possible competition for the kill, not threat to me.** This is a plausible read for an Expert Hunter; it's also incomplete.

The cougar's actual mechanical state (which Ren cannot see):
- `hunger-state` HIGH (poor hunting + storm disrupted normal patterns)
- `territory-status` patrol (drainage 3 is its range)
- `behavioral-memory.this-human-presence` updating across past 3 days
- `personality.opportunism` HIGH (this individual is bold)
- It has been within 200m of Ren on Days 13, 14, 17 — she did not detect it

By 14:30, Ren is closing on the caribou herd. Wind still favorable. Crouches and moves slowly through brush. Kneels behind a deadfall log, places arrow on string, eases up to look over.

She places the arrow on the lead doe.

**Body cold-cough triggers — she involuntarily coughs hard.** Caribou heads snap up. Lead doe is alert. Ren has seconds to release. She does. Arrow strikes the doe in the rear hindquarter — a wound, not vital. Caribou herd flees east. Ren is committed to recovery.

**Body state**: respiratory cough has just cost her a kill. Now she has a wounded animal to track. The day is fading. ~3 hours of usable light.

### Beat 27 — Day 19, the cougar

**Day 19, dawn.** Ren tracking the wounded caribou through soft ground. Blood trail intermittent, slow recovery. She's traveled 4km from camp following sign that bedded for 6 hours overnight, then resumed travel.

**Mid-morning, ~10:30**, she's bent over examining a soft-ground impression that suggests the caribou bedded recently very close. She's in mixed thicket, head down.

The cougar's mechanical alignment is now critical:
- Has been tracking Ren since Day 13 (territorial patrol + observed-human pattern)
- Has been hungry through the storm
- Has watched her wound the caribou yesterday — followed the blood trail too
- Is now within 30m of Ren, in cover, downwind, watching
- Utility AI weights: hunger HIGH × isolation HIGH × cover HIGH × prey-vulnerability (head-down, distracted) HIGH × prior-experience-with-this-human (has tracked her, no negative outcome) → predatory-attack output selected

**The pounce.**

Ren has no warning. Cougars are stealth-stalking predators; their attack window is 1-2 seconds from launch to bite. She's bent over a track in dense cover. She does not hear, does not see, does not have time to draw bow.

Cougar from above and behind. Bite to back of neck — cervical vertebrae targeted. **§15 lethality model: vital strike, fatal.**

Ren collapses. She is conscious for ~10 seconds, dimly aware of weight and warmth and the smell of blood. Then she is not.

The cougar drags her ~80m to a denser thicket and begins to feed.

### Beat 28 — Aftermath (in-world consequences)

The world continues without Ren. Mechanical state:

- **Cougar** — fed for the first time in 5 days; behavioral memory updates: "human in territory, takeable." This individual will continue to opportunistically hunt humans if conditions align in future.
- **Caribou herd** — the doe was wounded but not lethal; she'll heal slowly, become more vigilant.
- **Drainage 3** — Ren's snares remain set. Hare in trap will likely be claimed by foxes or coyotes within hours. Compendium back-room (in Ren's player account) preserves all entries: cougar sign, storm survival, the wounded caribou, the final tracking job.
- **Ren's body** — at the kill site, becomes carrion. **§13.2 Carrion Chains activates** — scent draws scavengers. Ravens within hours, coyotes within a day, the cougar holding territory for several days.

## What ended Ren — mechanical reading

The death emerged from these conditions aligning:

1. **Cumulative isolation** — 19 days alone without rest from social cohesion or rotation
2. **Respiratory cough from storm exposure** — caused the cough that startled the caribou, leading to the wound, leading to the long-tracking-pursuit
3. **Caribou wound** — extended her into deeper drainage-3 cougar territory than she'd otherwise have gone
4. **Cougar's accumulated hunger** — drainage 3 was thin during the storm; cougar's predatory-attack weight had been rising
5. **Cougar's behavioral memory of Ren** — repeated low-consequence detection across days; "this human is takeable" rose
6. **The specific moment** — head down, no cover, no peripheral vision, focused on tracking
7. **Cougar's stealth** — Ren's predator-reading is good for bears and wolves; cougars are uniquely undetectable; Expert tier didn't compensate

No single thing killed her. The cascade did. **This is what "mechanical death" looks like in a system without scripted outcomes.**

## Architecture stress-test results (Run 01 full)

- ✅ **§1 No Dominant Strategy** held under pressure. Bow Hunter's loss of cache to bear was the right cost; rifle Hunter would have had different cost (sound broadcast)
- ✅ **§2 Mechanical Behavior** held throughout. No event was scripted; every consequence emerged from inputs aligning
- ✅ **§13.2 Carrion Chains** fired exactly as designed — bear's arrival was inevitable from scent + apex setup; not random
- ✅ **§13.3 Tracks system** held — wounded recovery, weather erasure pressure, substrate-aware decay all present
- ✅ **§14 Skill System** stress-tested in full. Debuff-removal model produced felt differences. Mastery + novelty multiplier worked.
- ✅ **§15 Combat & Weapons**: bow + lethality + apex defense all functional. Wound mode fired naturally (G39 surfaced as needed amendment).
- ❗ **G39 needed for §15 to not produce 100%-reliable Expert kills** — animal-movement-at-shot-release variable required to honor No Dominant Strategy
- ❗ **Cougar fairness gap** — cougar lethality without G9 movement modes + vision cone + self-state visibility produced an unsurvivable encounter for an Expert player. Counterplay surface depends on multiple unresolved gaps.

## Big takeaways from Run 01

1. **Spring forest baseline isn't dramatic but reveals plenty.** No crisis, ~3 in-game hours, 10 gaps + 3 collisions in Day 1 alone.
2. **The §12.3 combat decision was unblockable for Hunter design.** Resolved in §15.
3. **Movement modes are huge** — every encounter from hour 1 hinged on stalk-vs-walk-vs-stop, sound discipline, downwind positioning. Load-bearing system, not yet spec'd at all (G9).
4. **The bear-takes-the-cache moment is the kind of story the design is for.** Not "you fail," not "you win" — "you got something, you lost something, you're alive, the bear ate well."
5. **Apex individual identity (G25) just paid off.** "The bear of the meadow" is now a recurring antagonist for Ren. Long-arc storytelling without scripting.
6. **Death must emerge mechanically.** Ren's cougar-death is the proof: a cascade of mechanical state alignments, none of it designed for dramatic effect. This is the genre's commitment in action.
