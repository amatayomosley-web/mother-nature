# Run 08 — Builder, Forest, Mid-October Solo Arrival (Cold-Onset Rush)

Date of run: 2026-05-10
Status: Day 23 in-game, character alive but compromised; winter survival uncertain
Run author: William + Claude (design exercise)

## Setup

- **Character**: "Kai" (placeholder name) — late 30s, Builder node (Carpenter primary, Mason baseline), Survivalist strong-secondary
- **Biome**: Mixed forest at moderate elevation (~700m); deciduous already half-bare, conifers dominant on the upper slopes
- **Season**: Mid-October. ~21 days estimated before sustained snow + reliable sub-freezing nights. First hard frosts already firing on clear nights at his elevation.
- **Weather at start**: Cool overcast, ~6°C day / ~1°C night, light NW wind, ground wet from recent rain. Trees fully shedding.
- **Scenario**: Flash flood washed out a creek crossing during a hiking party traverse. Group scattered downstream; Kai is solo and uninjured but separated from the bulk of the gear (group tent, two-person saw, the felling axe, the bulk-food bag). He's still on the planned side of the divide but with what fit in his pack only.
- **Time at start**: Mid-afternoon Day 1.

### Starting kit (truncated by accident, not by design)

Hatchet, belt knife, multi-tool, 2.5×3m tarp, bedroll + 3-season sleeping bag (rated ~-5°C — *not* a winter bag), 7 days preserved food, 4L water capacity, fire kit (ferro rod + lighter + tinder), 15m paracord, bolt-action rifle (.30-06 class) + 10 rounds, folded 50cm saw blade (no handle — bow-saw frame was in the lost group gear), 1.5L steel pot, headlamp. Clothing on body: wool base, fleece mid, hardshell, gloves, watch cap, hikers, gaiters. **No insulated parka. No second pad.**

### Compendium grants at start

- Expert: shelter-construction, joinery, fortification, tool-craft, food-cache, fire-craft (Builder spans into fire because hearths are construction)
- Practiced: cold-management, knot-craft, basic carpentry-in-cold, weather signs, basic plant-ID (Survivalist cross-domain)
- Novice: hunting, tracking, butchery, hide-curing
- Glimpsed: predator-reading, medicinal plants

### Skill node

Builder (Carpenter primary). Master-only signature abilities available in domain: **smoke-vented winter cabin**, **raised flood-tolerant platform** (§14.5).

### Goal

Reach the trailhead in ~21–25 days, OR construct survivable winter shelter on-site and overwinter. No prearranged extraction; nobody is coming. The party will assume the worst by Day 6.

## Day 1 — triage, arrival math, the first axe-only inventory

### Beat 01 — 14:30, the inventory

Kai sits on a downed log on a south-facing bench and does the math cold:

- **7 days food / ~21 days to cold** = half-rations for 14 days, 80% replacement from the land
- **No felling axe** = hatchet ceiling ~12–15cm trees; bigger costs murderous calories
- **No saw handle** = folded blade is dead weight until he carves a frame (tomorrow)
- **3-season bag** = below ~0°C he is in compounding sleep debt unless the shelter does the insulation work
- **Rifle, 10 rounds** = ~3 big-game kills, plus defense margin. Every shot a drainage-wide broadcast (§15.4)

**§14.13 saliency** at Builder Expert fires hard on this bench: slope, drainage, sun-exposure, wind from the bowed treetops, rock outcrop ~40m up-slope, spring seep ~80m below, blowdown stand south. He doesn't think the word "saliency"; he sees the building.

**Decision by 15:15**: build here. 25km of trailless ground in worsening weather with a 3-season bag is worse than a known site with three weeks to work.

**Need**: N46 — Triage arithmetic mental model. The ration-math-vs-days-to-cold calc is this scenario's central decision; must be supported without becoming a spreadsheet.

### Beat 02 — 15:30, rough-and-now shelter

One-hour expedient lean-to: tarp lashed to two saplings, rock as rear wall (thermal mass + windbreak), spruce boughs as bed, deadfall front weight-line. **Quality grade C**. He knows it's a C; he built it for tonight.

Small fire 2m off the rock, reflector wall from three wedged slabs. Triples felt heat without burning more wood. **§14.5 master-only "smoke-vented winter cabin" does NOT apply yet** — open-fire, no chimney. Smoke broadcasts.

Cold ration (saves fuel). Sleep.

### Beat 03 — Night 1

Sleep grade C-D. Bag at its rating, pad thin, ground sucks heat. Wakes 02:00 to a coyote yip ~600m off — auto-log fires. Stokes. Back. By 06:00 finger-dexterity flag firing (cold-driven, same mechanism as exhaustion shake). Net recovery ~60% — short of the 80% he'd need for a full work day.

**Note**: at this debt level the game has not yet degraded his perception. The §14.13 spec says it should start to soon. Flagged below as N54.

## Days 2–4 — the saw handle, the shelter decision, the calorie debt opening

### Beat 04 — Day 2 morning, the saw handle

The folded saw blade is the multiplier — with it, ~3× cross-cut speed and cleaner notches. A bow-saw frame is two saplings + crosspiece + paracord tension + tensioner stick. ~90 minutes for a Builder Expert, **grade A frame**.

The cost is 90 minutes of *now*. Every pre-cold minute is his most valuable. He builds it anyway. Pays back inside Day 3.

**Need**: N47 — Tool improvisation recipes as named craftables with time/quality costs.

### Beat 05 — Day 2, shelter triage tree

He runs the decision tree out loud:

**A — Lean-to closed up.** Day 1's lean-to expanded to three-sided cabin against rock, leaf+bough insulation, reflector hearth. ~5–6 days. Marginal at -10°C; dangerous below -20°C. Fast, drafty, fuel-hungry.

**B — Half-dugout in slope.** Excavate ~80cm into south-facing slope, log-and-bough roof, banked walls, central hearth. ~10–14 days. Adequate at -20°C. Best thermal — but one shovel-equivalent (multi-tool + hatchet flat) and frozen ground starts ~Day 14. The dig is the gamble.

**C — Deadfall + rock-overhang composite.** Partial natural overhang he didn't fully read yesterday. Stack deadfall front, pack gaps, hearth at front lip. ~3–4 days. Surprisingly good at -10°C (rock thermal mass + natural roof). At -20°C wind-dependent. Location-locked.

**D — Progressive: lean-to → composite (Week 1) → half-banked walls + proper hearth (Week 2-3).** Each layer shippable.

**Kai picks D, anchored to C's overhang.** Builder Expert reads the rock stability (no recent rockfall, no fresh face on stones, stable root-mat above) — a novice would not commit; an Expert sees the cues. No milled boards. Round-pole only. No level, no plumb beyond Builder Expert eyeball (trained-eyeball-plumb debuff removed; walls a few degrees off true; doesn't matter for thermal).

### Beat 06 — Day 2 evening, the first tree

Fells a 14cm spruce with hatchet alone — 35 minutes, would be 8 with a felling axe. Clean cut, drop on line. Trims with saw. The saw earns its 90 minutes back in one afternoon.

Cooked dehydrated meal. Day 2 net **deficit -2800 cal**. Fine for one day. The pattern is the worry.

### Beat 07 — Day 3, first courses

Course 1 of 4 cabin-shell logs laid. Saddle notch with hatchet is ~15 min/notch where a felling-axe master would do 4. *Quality* grade A; *time* grade C. **§14.3 production grading with the action-time axis hit hard but the grade axis spared.**

Sets two snares (Novice trapping; he doesn't expect much).

**Need**: N48 — Tool-tier modulating action-time independently of output-quality. Builder Expert with wrong tools = slow, not bad. This is the heart of the triage scenario.

### Beat 08 — Day 4, the rifle question

Hunger 45%. Net-negative every day. Snares empty.

Carries rifle on a scout. Finds fresh deer tracks, a small-buck rub line, a bedding depression. Sits at meadow edge ~75 minutes. **No deer comes.**

Walks back, runs the rifle math: a shot broadcasts ~2-3km. Every coyote and bear in those drainages learns there's a human and a kill site. He'd defend a carcass against scavengers while he should be cutting wood. **The rifle isn't an opportunistic-hunting tool here** — it's one calculated kill late in the food window and one defensive shot if predators press camp. Back on the rack.

**§1 held**: the rifle is powerful AND the cost of using it badly is huge. The cost-matrix in §15.4 prices restraint without scolding the player.

**Need**: N49 — Sound-broadcast cost model in construction-prep scenarios.

## Days 5–10 — Week 1, the shell, the cut, the snare hit

### Beat 09 — Day 5, the saw slip

Bucking a 12cm downed birch, kneeling, blade lengthwise. The log shifts on the slope. The saw skips out of the kerf and his off-hand — steadying the log — catches the blade across the back of the second knuckle.

**~3cm cut, back of hand.** Bleeds steady; tendons intact. §22 / §15 wounded-self — wound mode, not vital. Cleans with boiled water (kit has bandages + painkiller; no antiseptic, no antibiotics). Splints finger to next.

Hand will work in 2–3 days for light use; hatchet power-swings out for ~5 days. **No HUD** — what he feels is bandage tightness, throb, and the *avoidance* his body hands him when he reaches for the hatchet.

**Need**: N50 — Wound that costs technique, not stamina. Builder Expert must lose specific actions (power-swing hatchet, two-handed saw) without losing the broader debuff stack.

### Beat 10 — Days 5–7, the slow days

One-handed work: lashing, knife-cutting boughs, gathering, sorting timber. Stacks bough mat. Carves splitting wedges. Sets the snare line wider (8 snares, Novice). Builds a downwind food cache, hatchet-hung at the right bear-defeat height — Builder's cache-placement debuff removed (the tree he picks is not the closest, it's the right one).

**Snare hit Day 7**: snowshoe hare, ~1200 cal. Field-dresses Novice — 30 min, grade C (an Expert Hunter: 8 min, grade A). Half hot tonight, half smoked over the reflector ~24 hours.

**First net-positive day Day 7.** Won't be the week's pattern.

### Beat 11 — Days 8–10, Week 1 shell

By dusk Day 10: three of four log courses up; ridge poles in, rafters stacked; deadfall composite front with loose moss/bough chinking; 30cm hearth pit lined with flat stones, reflector behind, fire venting cleanly up the overhang lip. Hand closed, ~80% hatchet-capable.

Body Day 10 evening: warmth 70%, hunger 50%, thirst 80%, tiredness 75% (cumulative debt visible). Cautiously focused. The shell is real; a master-grade hearth-vented cabin won't ship in 21 days, but a survivable Week 2 shelter will.

## Days 11–17 — Week 2, the roof, the cold tipping, the first deer

### Beat 12 — Day 11, the freeze

Ice in the pot — 2cm cap, not a skim. Overnight ~-4°C; the shell broke wind but the open hearth side leaked. **§13.4 wet wood is the new daily problem** — deadfall no longer drying between rains. He triages dry-only fuel under the partial roof.

Moves the pot to the hearth lip nightly. Rigs an awkward second reservoir from a paracord-hung mylar wrap.

**Need**: N51 — Water-storage-in-cold mechanic. Pots freeze; volumes need to live near heat; melt-fuel cost is real.

### Beat 13 — Days 11–13, the roof + second hare

Closes the roof: round-pole rafters lashed and notched; 30cm bough mat; tarp pressed into service as vapor barrier; second mat on top. **Grade B roof** for round-pole-no-mill — the mat/vapor/mat layering is trained joinery, not novice intuition.

Day 12 a second hare (~1200 cal) — smokes it grade B, ~6-day shelf. Day 13 a half-pound of squirrel-cached nuts (Practiced plant-ID, raids a hollow, takes half — the rest feeds the squirrel that may feed a marten that may trip a snare).

### Beat 14 — Day 14, the deer

Late afternoon, Kai is sawing roof boards (novelty-trickle XP firing on bucksaw technique) when a young white-tail doe steps from brush 30m off. Wind in his face. She's looking *up the slope*, not at him.

Rifle's leaning at the doorframe 6m off. The five seconds. Sets the saw down in one motion. Four steps. Lifts. Sights. Breathes out. Squeezes.

**Vital hit, lung.** §15 hit-zone. Doe runs ~25m and collapses. The report rolls out across two drainages; the echo comes back a full second later off the ridge.

Field-dressing Novice: ~75 minutes grade C. Loins, backstraps, heart, liver — 10kg packed; ~25kg + hide hung 4.5m on a sapling meat-pole (40 minutes for the hang alone). Back at camp full-dark.

**§13.2 Carrion Chains firing** — scent downwind. Coyotes by dawn likely; a still-up bear within 24–48 hours possible (late October — most denning, not all).

End Day 14: warmth 75%, hunger 75%, tired 70%, morale high. ~10 days of meat if he can keep it.

### Beat 15 — Day 15, the cache war

Dawn: hung carcass intact (bears denning, no triangulation yet) but gut pile gone (coyotes) and fox tracks circling. Packs the rest out in two trips. All meat at the cabin by dusk, smoking on a proper rack between saplings. Hide cured with wood ash as poor-man's salt — Practiced hide-curing grade D; door curtain, not parka.

**Needs**:
- N52 — Wood-ash cure (hides + light antiseptic) underspecified
- N53 — Bear-denning window mechanic. Late-October encounter probability measurably lower than mid-September; the world should model seasonal predator phase-out.

### Beat 16 — Days 16–17, banked walls

Bank-earths the downwind wall — dirt + leaf litter + bough against the lower 60cm of log, packed and tamped. Thermal mass is real now. Interior holds 4–6°C above ambient overnight (against 1–2°C previously).

**No chimney.** Hearth still vents through the overhang lip. The master signature "smoke-vented winter cabin" requires master-tier (§14.5); he is Builder Expert. **The chimney he cannot build is felt as absence.** Smoke is going to bite when the wind shifts.

## Days 18–23 — Week 3, the first snow, the smoke, the cold-debt cascade

### Beat 17 — Day 18, the snow

First sustained snow mid-morning, ~5cm by evening, ~10cm by dawn Day 19. -7°C overnight. The 3-season bag is at the edge of its rating; he sleeps in his clothing layers and stays warm-enough. **Sleep grade C-B.** Net debt still accumulating.

**§14.13 saliency under sustained debt starts to register here.** The *high-priority* cues — wind smell, cold on hands, fire state — remain sharp. The *low-priority* cues — squirrel chatter that would have flagged the marten near the smoke rack; the slight bough-mat discoloration that means damp; the saw blade tension drifting — fade. He doesn't notice the marten Day 18. He doesn't notice the bough damp Day 19. He does notice the wind shift.

**Need**: N54 — **Stress-modulated saliency**. Currently §14.13 specs *skill*-modulated only. Under cumulative sleep + caloric + cold debt, the saliency window should narrow — high-priority cues sharpen, low-priority cues fade. Same shader pipeline; second parameter.

### Beat 18 — Day 19, the marten

Dawn: ~1.5kg of partly-smoked deer is gone. Drag-trail; half-eaten chunk in a hollow log 30m off. Recovers ~0.5kg spoiled (boils hard, eats against better judgment — grade D safety, he is hungry).

This is the "missed the Day 18 cue" cascade closing. Moves the rack inside the doorway — losing smoke efficiency for security. Builder correction: *the food belongs inside the wall by Week 3*.

### Beat 19 — Day 20, the wind shift

Wind to NE. The hearth now vents up the *upwind* lip. Smoke pools at the back of the cabin and stings his eyes within ten minutes. He sleeps with the door propped a hand's width — heat for breathable air.

**Wind-tied quality grade**: shelter is grade C in this wind direction, grade B in the previous. A non-master shelter has wind directions it's good in and bad in. A master's chimney would solve it (locked behind tier).

**Need**: N56 — Wind-direction-tied shelter quality grade.

### Beat 20 — Days 21–23, the cold-onset window meets the prepared shelter

Day 21 dawn -10°C. Day 22 -12°C. Day 23 -9°C with snow. The cascade is here.

Day 23 status:
- **Calories**: ~6 days smoked deer (less marten loss), 2 days original rations, small nut cache
- **Fuel**: ~10 days dry hard-deadfall under the partial-roof annex. §13.4 wet wood discipline load-bearing — no damp-fuel fires since the snow.
- **Water**: pot melts snow nightly. Sustainable.
- **Shelter**: half-banked, roof-tarped, overhang-fronted. Reliable -5°C to -10°C. -15°C with the right wind. Untested below.
- **Body**: warmth 70% (bag is the limit, not the cabin), hunger 55%, tiredness 70%, hands stiff at dawn from half-cured cold. Wound closed; small scar.
- **Tools**: hatchet sharp, saw re-tensioned Day 22 (caught the wear after smoke broke his routine), rifle clean, 9 rounds.

**The honest read**: more than Day 1's lean-to; less than a master Builder's cabin with the felling axe and the partner. Food ~8 days. Fuel ~10 days. One bad week from cascade. One good kill from cushion.

**Alive at Day 23. Run could go either way.**

## Architecture stress-test results (Run 08 through Day 23)

- ✅ **§1 No Dominant Strategy under triage**: rifle restraint, shelter-option triage, saw-handle-now-vs-cut-now — each path had a real cost. The rifle is powerful AND broadcasts; the dugout is thermally best AND too slow; the lean-to is fastest AND drafts; the composite is middle-ground AND location-locked. No path dominant.

- ✅ **§2 Mechanical behavior**: nothing scripted. The hare hit the snare because hares used that run. The deer stepped out because Kai was quiet and the wind was right. The marten took the meat because the rack was outdoors and unattended. The smoke pooled because the wind shifted.

- ✅ **§14 debuff-removal + mastery delta under triage**: Builder Expert's debuff-removal visible in every joinery decision, cache placement, wind read. The non-Builder debuffs (hunting, tracking, hide-curing) visible as *time and grade costs* — deer field-dress 75 min grade C vs Expert Hunter 25 min grade A. **Same scenario, different node, different game.**

- ✅ **§14.5 master-only gated correctly**: Kai is Builder Expert, not Master. Smoke-vented winter cabin is locked. The chimney would have closed a real gap. Felt as absence.

- ✅ **§15 combat / hit-zone**: one rifle shot, one vital, one drainage-wide broadcast. Cost-matrix played out. If a bear had still been up in the drainage, the run probably ends differently.

- ✅ **§13.4 wet wood**: load-bearing. Day 11 freeze made the discipline visible. Stockpile-under-roof is the diegetic UI.

- ✅ **§13.2 Carrion Chains** + bear-denning window: chains fired at the kill site; the bear that didn't arrive (because it was denning) is a *negative observation* — the player learns the seasonal predator phase by what *didn't* happen.

- ❗ **§14.13 stress-modulated saliency is the load-bearing gap of this run.** Currently §14.13 specs skill-modulation only. The Day 18 marten miss + Day 19 loss are stress-modulation manifesting. Recommend: high stress *boosts* high-priority cues (predator nearby, immediate cold) and *attenuates* low-priority cues (squirrel chatter, bough damp). Same shader pipeline, second parameter.

- ❗ **§14.3 quality grading needs a tool-tier axis.** Today: skill → (grade, time). Missing: skill × tool → (grade, time, calorie-cost). Builder Expert with no felling axe builds same-grade notch in 4× the time. Builder Novice with felling axe builds a faster notch of worse grade. Surfaces across every construction action.

- ❗ **Cold-onset cascade visibility**: mechanically correct but compounding curve is implicit. Body grade felt, sleep debt accumulates, cold-tolerance falls — but the *cascade* is not surfaced. Recommend: diegetic surfacing through morning-mutter frequency tied to cumulative debt, not a HUD bar.

## What ended Kai (forecast, not yet realized)

The run is open at Day 23. Two paths fork visibly:

**Path 1 — Survives the first hard week.** If: one more big-game kill or sustained snare line; weather window for chinking + chimney attempt; no serious second wound; no predator pressing camp (rifle reserve covers one defensive shot). Then Kai overwinters in a survivable-not-master's cabin. Emerges in March with a story.

**Path 2 — The cascade closes.** If any of: second injury during fast hatchet work in cold-stiffened hands; storm before chinking cures (mold + draft + wrong-wind smoke); a bear that doesn't den; a wolf pack drawn by the smoke-rack scent + kill-site memory; sleep debt > 5 nights below grade-C tipping into degraded judgment (per N54). Then Kai dies in the next 14 days. **Most likely death signature**: cold-cascade compounded by injury or debt-degraded judgment — kin to Ari's Run 02 death but in a forest, in a shelter that nearly worked.

The run is honest in not resolving. The architecture surfaces both paths from real mechanical state.

## Big takeaways from Run 08

1. **Triage Builder is a different game from leisurely Builder.** Run 03's Tova had 30 days, full tools, deliberate site — and died of an axe slip + infection. Kai has 21 days, half the tools, an emergency arrival — and might survive. The variable is not gear; it is **scope discipline** and the master/Expert/Novice gradient against the chosen scope.

2. **Master vs Expert delta under triage is real and felt.** The chimney he cannot build, the dugout he didn't dare, the rifle restraint he held — each is the Master's gap. Roughly 2 extra survival days from the "smoke-vented winter cabin" signature alone; possibly 4 from better joinery-speed calorie efficiency. §14.5 master-only is the right discrimination for this scenario.

3. **§14.13 needs stress-modulation.** Day 18-19 marten loss is the demonstrative case. Same shader pipeline, second parameter.

4. **Tool-tier separated from skill-tier is the missing axis on §14.3.** `skill × tool → (grade, time, calorie-cost)`, not `skill → (grade, time)` with tool collapsed in.

5. **Cold-onset cascade is mechanically correct, but its compound visibility is implicit.** Individual debts felt; cascade nature invisible. Diegetic mutter-frequency tied to cumulative debt, not a HUD bar.

6. **The rifle is conserved by its own cost, not by the player.** Across Runs 01, 02, 08: used twice, held back ~dozen times. §15.4's cost-matrix prices the tool into restraint without scolding.

7. **The "shelter that nearly worked" is the right Week-3 outcome for Expert under constraint.** Not the master's cabin; not the novice's failed lean-to. Weather-tight three-sided composite with banked walls, wind-direction-dependent grade, and a smoke problem on shift days. The **felt shape of competence under constraint** — what playthroughs are for.
