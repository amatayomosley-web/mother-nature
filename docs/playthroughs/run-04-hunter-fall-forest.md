# Run 04 — Hunter, Forest, Mid-Autumn (Rut Season)

Date of run: 2026-05-10
Status: Day 10 in-game, character died (trampled by bull moose during rut)
Run author: William + Claude (design exercise)

## Setup

- **Character**: "Sam" (placeholder name) — Hunter node
- **Biome**: Forest, mid-autumn (deciduous turning + falling; ground covered in leaf litter; days shortening)
- **Season**: Late October equivalent — peak moose rut; deer in early rut; cool nights (~-2°C), mild days (~8°C)
- **Scenario**: Planned 10-day big-game hunting expedition. Sam came specifically for the rut — bulls are vulnerable during this period (aggressive but distracted; bugling reveals their location).
- **Time at start**: Mid-morning Day 1

### Starting kit

- Hunter standard: bow + 8 arrows + knife
- Hand axe, flint-and-steel, canteen 1L, cordage 10m, 6 snares
- Wool blanket, oiled tarp 2×3m
- 3 days preserved food
- Fall-specific: heavier wool layered (anticipating night cold drops), wool gloves, brimmed hat, spare bowstring + fletching kit, basic hide-curing supplies (salt + small amount of brain-tan oil)
- Bone moose-call (rut-specific tool: imitates cow moose calling a bull)

### Compendium grants at start

- Expert: deer, rabbit, bear, wolf, fox, grouse, common tracks, field-dressing, basic predator behavior, **moose** (Hunter Expert covers moose biology, rut behavior, anatomy for shot placement)
- Practiced: edible autumn plants (mushrooms — narrower set than spring; late roots; nuts), basic weather signs, hide-curing
- Novice: medicinal plants, distant predator calls
- Glimpsed: rarer fauna

### Skill node

Hunter — tracking, predator-reading, field-dressing, butchery, stalking

### Goal

Bag a bull moose. Pack out the highest-value cuts + hide. Return home by Day 10 with rut-season trophy.

## Days 1-2 — site selection + base camp

### Beat 01 — Day 1, hike in + camp

Sam hikes 5 hours from trailhead. Settles in a mid-elevation forest valley with a small creek, mixed birch + pine + maple, with sightlines opening to the south where a meadow edge meets the forest.

**§14.13 saliency** fires: she catches caribou-track-equivalents at the meadow edge (no caribou this season — but elk or moose). Fresh tubular scat on the trail — moose dropping, ~12 hours old. She's near the target.

Sets camp on a rise ~100m back from the meadow edge, downwind of the prevailing southwest breeze. Tarp + boughs + dry-stocked fuel from autumn deadfall (abundant — leaves cover everything but the deadfall is generally drier than spring forest).

### Beat 02 — Day 2, scouting + base routines

Walks 2km in three directions. Finds:
- Multiple rut signs: rubbed trees (bark scraped from antler-rubbing, ~chest-high; bull marks territory); a wallow (mud pit ~3m across, fresh — bull bathing in own urine to attract cows); droppings in multiple spots
- Fresh deer tracks (early rut for them too; bucks now territorial but less dangerous than bull moose)
- No bear sign in this immediate area; no wolf sign
- A small autumn berry patch (Practiced edibility — supplements rations)

Sets 3 snares on rabbit runs near camp. Sam's plan is moose-focused but small-game backup is wise.

End of Day 2: body grade-A condition, full rations, camp solid.

## Days 3-5 — finding the bull

### Beat 03 — Day 3, listening + scouting

Pre-dawn (~05:30), Sam wakes for the cool-time scout. Bull moose call most actively dawn and dusk. She walks to a rise within hearing range of the meadow and waits.

**At 06:15**, distant — perhaps 2km off — a deep moaning call. **Bull moose bugle (rut-call).** Sam identifies direction: NE.

She crosses 1.5km toward the call over the next 2 hours. Reaches a creek bottom where moose sign concentrates — fresh tracks, mud-disturbance from a wallow, antler-rub on a young pine ~chest-high.

Bull's territory confirmed. He's ranging this drainage. She marks the area mentally, returns to camp by mid-morning to plan.

### Beat 04 — Day 4, refining the location

Mid-day scout into the bull's drainage. **Finds his bedding area** — a depression in low brush, ~4m across, with hair and feces marking it. Recently used. Bull beds here mid-day.

Reading: he's a substantial bull. Hoof prints ~14cm long (large adult; possibly 600-700kg). Antler-rub height suggests a 5+ year old.

**§14.13 saliency at Expert Hunter level** reads all this clearly. A Novice would see "bedding area" and miss most of the inference.

She backs out without disturbing. Plans her hunt for tomorrow or day after — wants the bull settled into routine; her approach calculated.

### Beat 05 — Day 5, the cow encounter

Morning scout. Sam sees a cow moose at the meadow edge, ~120m off. The cow is calm, browsing. Sam stays in cover; doesn't approach. **The cow signals bull presence** — bulls during rut stay near cows or in their travel paths.

Sam watches for 30 minutes. Cow moves north into the trees. Sam doesn't follow; instead returns to camp.

Plans the hunt for tomorrow at dawn. The wallow is the key — bulls visit wallows pre-dawn for cooling + chemistry. If she can position downwind of the wallow at first light, she can ambush at close range.

## Days 6-8 — refining the approach

### Beat 06 — Day 6, the failed pre-dawn

Sam at the wallow position pre-dawn (~05:30). Downwind, in dense brush, motionless. Cold (~-1°C). She waits ~3 hours.

**Bull doesn't come.** He may have visited earlier (before her arrival) or skipped today. By 08:30 she leaves the position; the warming day makes the bull less likely to come now.

Returns to camp at 10:00. Eats. Plans next attempt for tomorrow with adjustment — arrive earlier (04:30).

### Beat 07 — Day 7, weather + cow-calling

Wind shifts northerly during the night. Sam reconsiders her wallow position — now no longer downwind. She'd need a different approach.

Decides to try a different tactic: **the moose-call**. She has a bone cow-call. A rut-bull will respond to a cow-call by approaching to investigate. She finds a clearing ~600m from the bedding area, sets up in cover, calls.

**No response.** She tries again every 20 minutes for 2 hours. Nothing. Either the bull isn't within hearing or he's already with a cow and not seeking another.

Returns to camp by mid-day. Body grade-B (cold morning, some fatigue), morale slightly down — 5 days of hunting without a kill.

### Beat 08 — Day 8, the bull's rub-line

Sam walks the bull's likely travel route — found rub-line connecting the bedding area to the wallow, ~800m of forest. She follows the rub-line, finds fresh tracks (this morning).

**Bull is here today.** She tracks ~1km along the line, slowly, ready. **Active skill: stalking.** Wind in her face. **Passive: scent-suppression.**

Around 14:00, she catches sight of a brown shape through trees, ~70m off. **The bull.** He's standing, ears alert. He's heard or seen something — possibly her, possibly something else.

She freezes. The bull's body is angled mostly away from her, head turning. **Saliency-Expert assessment of bull state**: he's not yet committed to flee or charge. He's checking.

**Window: she can either shoot now at 70m (long for bow, possible vital but high miss-or-wound risk) or back off and try again.**

She backs off. The bull, after a long minute, turns and walks away through trees. She breathes out. Marks the position for tomorrow.

## Days 9-10 — the kill attempt

### Beat 09 — Day 9, closing in

Morning. Sam approaches the bull's known bedding area with intent to wait for him to lie down. Wind from south, favorable.

**At 09:30**, she finds his fresh tracks heading toward the wallow — odd, since wallows are dawn/dusk. Maybe he's restless today (rut behavior — bulls move erratically when seeking cows).

She follows. Hears him at the wallow before she sees him — splash, low moan. **At 50m**, through dense brush, she sees him at the wallow. He's standing in the mud, head lowered.

Cover is thick. Wind holds favorable. She maneuvers to 35m — ideal bow range — kneels behind a deadfall. **Heart rate elevated.** Cracked-rib-equivalent? No, she's healthy — just adrenaline.

The bull is partially obscured by saplings. She has a partial broadside shot at his lower chest — possibly heart, possibly stomach. Not the ideal vitals window (lung-heart triangle).

**She waits.** Five minutes. Bull moves slightly, gives her a cleaner angle. She draws the bow.

### Beat 10 — Day 10, the shot, the charge

She releases the arrow at 35m. Arrow strikes — **gut hit, not vital**. Per §15 hit-zone lethality, this is a wound, not a kill.

**Critical mechanical fact: this is a bull moose in rut, not a deer.**

A wounded deer flees. A wounded moose during rut — particularly a bull whose territorial-aggression weights are MAX — has a different response. **The bull charges the source of the threat.**

In 2 seconds, he's covered 25m. Sam has no time to climb the nearest tree (~15m away). She tries to step behind the deadfall for cover.

The bull crashes through brush. He's at her in 4 seconds total. **600+ kg of animal in full-charge.** Antlers + hooves + sheer mass.

She raises her bow as a defensive gesture — useless. The bull collides with the deadfall + her behind it. The deadfall log gives. He's on her. Antler-tine hits her chest. Hoof comes down on her ribs. Another hoof on her hip. Her body is broken across multiple major joints.

**She's not conscious past the first 2-3 seconds of contact.** The bull tramples and rolls over her body. He stands over her, blood-soaked, ears back, breathing hard.

After ~30 seconds the bull retreats from the area, agitated, leaking blood from the gut wound. He moves ~200m before bedding down.

He may or may not survive the gut wound. **Sam doesn't.**

### Beat 11 — Aftermath

The bull bleeds out over ~6 hours from the gut wound. He dies that evening ~200m from where he killed Sam.

**Two corpses, one drainage.** Wolves arrive within a day. The bull's larger body is the primary attractor. Sam's body — broken, smaller, leaking blood from multiple sites — becomes their second course.

A trapper, walking the area 5 days later, will find both bodies and reconstruct the scene from arrow recovery + hoofprints. Sam's compendium logs (back at her account) preserve all observations through her final shot.

**Field notes entry — moose-rut-death — would unlock for her player's next character** per §14.12 framework.

## What ended Sam — mechanical reading

The death emerged from these conditions aligning:

1. **Rut season** — bull moose during October has territorial-aggression weights at maximum; this is the natural state for the time of year
2. **Partial shot opportunity** — the cleaner vitals shot wasn't available; she had a partial-broadside lower-chest angle that was gut, not lung-heart
3. **Animal-movement-at-shot-release adjacent factor** — the bull's body angle wasn't ideal but stable; the wound went where the shot was placed, not where she'd aimed perfectly
4. **Wound mode triggered defensive-attack-aggression in rut bull**, not flee response. **§15 lethality model**: moose dimorphic threat — prey species that retains defensive-attack capability, especially during rut.
5. **Bow-to-charging-moose at 25m** — bow can't reload + fire in time; bow's cost-matrix shows this clearly (bow great for ambush at distance; bad for close-range defense)
6. **Climbing tree was too slow** — she had ~4 seconds total; not enough to reach 15m to nearest climbable
7. **Hide-behind-cover didn't hold** — the bull's mass + speed broke through the deadfall

No single thing killed her. The cascade — rut-state + partial shot + defensive-aggression + close-range bow limitations + insufficient escape time — did.

**Distinct death signature: prey-species defensive-attack during reproductive aggression period.** Different from Run 01 (predator-pounce), Run 02 (cold-cascade), Run 03 (injury-infection). Four death signatures across four runs; all mechanically honest.
