# Run 03 — Builder, Tundra, Late Autumn Expedition

Date of run: 2026-05-10
Status: Day 21 in-game, character died (sepsis from infected wound)
Run author: William + Claude (design exercise)

## Setup

- **Character**: "Tova" (placeholder name) — Builder node
- **Biome**: Tundra (lower elevation than Run 02; more shrub-tundra than alpine)
- **Season**: Late autumn turning toward winter; ~-5°C ambient at start, dropping over the month
- **Scenario**: Planned 30-day construction expedition to scout for a permanent cabin site
- **Time at start**: Mid-morning Day 1

### Starting kit (Builder, deliberate expedition)

- Builder standard: hand axe, knife, spear
- Construction tools: 2-handed bucksaw, hand auger, hammer + ~50 nails, spade
- Camp tools: 3×4m heavy tarp, 30m heavy cordage, smaller cordage 10m
- Bow + 12 arrows (Builder cross-domain Practiced bow handling — not Hunter Expert)
- 14 days emergency rations (planned for 30-day trip with foraging supplementing)
- Wool layered clothing, insulated boots, **proper down sleeping bag** (not mylar emergency)
- Fire-kit + 3 emergency flares + signal mirror + small medical kit (bandages, antiseptic, painkillers; no antibiotics)

### Compendium grants at start

- Expert: shelter-construction, fortification, tool-craft, food-cache, trap-defenses
- Practiced: cold-management (cross-domain Survivalist), fire-craft, basic carpentry, knot-craft
- Novice: hunting, plant-ID, tracking, weather-reading
- Glimpsed: predator-reading, advanced ecology, scent-reading

### Skill node

Builder — shelter-construction, food-cache, fortification, trap-defenses, tool-craft

### Goal

Build a defensible cabin shell over 30 days. Return home by Day 30. Or stay and complete a more permanent structure if conditions allow.

## Days 1-3 — site selection + base camp

### Beat 01 — Day 1, choosing the build site

Tova hikes 4 hours from drop-off point toward a low-relief area she scouted on a previous summer trip. The site she favors is on a slight rise with a south-facing aspect, ~80m from a small stream (water access), against a rock face on the north side (natural windbreak), with scattered dwarf birch and willow for materials.

**§14.13 saliency** fires in her domain: she sees the site as Builder Expert would — soil for stake-driving, slope for drainage, natural rock for foundation, sightlines clear to the north + east. A novice would just see "tundra."

Sets initial camp 30m from the build site: tarp + sleeping bag + small fire. Decides she'll move into the cabin shell once weather-tight.

### Beat 02 — Days 2-3, base camp routines

Day 2: foraging circuit (~3 hours, Builder Novice plant-ID — gathers some late-season lingonberry, a few mushrooms she's confident about, skips anything ambiguous). Builds outdoor latrine pit ~50m downwind. Surveys timber sources.

Day 3: Trial-fells a small birch with bucksaw — 40-min job, single tree, marks it for harvest tomorrow. **First mechanical surprise**: dwarf-tundra trees are SMALL (~3-4m, 15cm diameter). She'll need many more than expected for a substantial cabin.

**Need surfaced**:
- N38 — Tundra/biome-specific timber availability spec. The Builder's expectation of construction-grade timber doesn't match what's actually available in tundra. Forest biome would yield far more usable lumber per acre.

## Days 4-10 — timber + foundation

### Beat 03 — Days 4-6, intensive timber

Tova spends 3 days cutting and stacking small timber. Her bucksaw + axe combination cuts ~4 trees/day at her current skill. **Cumulative pack-out load** — she has to drag each log ~50-200m back to camp. Builder Expert means efficient cuts + reduced wear on tools, but the LOAD is the limit.

By end of Day 6 she has ~12 logs stacked. Original plan estimated needing ~40. **Calorie burn higher than planned.** Already eating into her 14-day ration supply at faster rate.

Days 4-6 ambient weather: -7°C average, no precipitation, light wind. Tolerable but not warming.

### Beat 04 — Days 7-9, foundation work

Begins foundation: clears a 3×4m level pad, drives stake corners, places 4 ground-runner logs. Uses 4 of her 50 nails. Discovers ground is frozen ~10cm down; auger work is much harder than expected. **Skill: Practiced cold-construction adjustment vs Expert summer-construction.** Adjusts pace.

By end of Day 9, foundation laid + 2 courses of wall logs placed. Cabin shape emerging. Still ~25 logs short.

**Need surfaced**:
- N39 — Frozen-ground construction mechanic. Cold-season Builder operates in fundamentally different conditions than warm-season. Tool wear, time-cost, structural choices all shift.

### Beat 05 — Day 10, the axe slip

Mid-day, Tova is felling a slightly larger birch (~5m, 20cm diameter — the biggest she's seen on site). The tree leans wrong as it falls; the trunk pinches her axe head into the cut. She works the axe free — pulling against the wedged trunk — and the axe slips out faster than expected. The blade glances down her left thigh.

**~6cm deep cut**, just above the knee. Hits flesh, misses major artery by luck. Bleeds significantly. She drops the axe, sits, applies pressure with her free hand and a rag from her pack.

**§22 injury system fires.** **§15 lethality (player wound mode)**: not vital strike, but a deep cut that requires real treatment.

She stops the bleeding after ~15 minutes of pressure. Cleans the wound at camp with antiseptic from her medical kit. Bandages tightly. Eats a meal. Decides to take 2 days rest before resuming work.

**Body state end of Day 10:**
- Warmth: 75% (fire alive)
- Hunger: 55%
- Thirst: 80%
- Tiredness: 60% (shock + blood loss)
- **Pain (leg wound): ~40% impairment**
- Injury risk: infection without monitoring

**Needs surfaced:**
- N40 — Axe slip / tool injury during construction work. Real wilderness risk.
- N41 — Wound treatment quality grading (per §14.3 — Practiced first-aid produces grade-B treatment; same supplies + Master would do grade-A).

## Days 11-15 — convalescence + infection

### Beat 06 — Days 11-13, slow recovery

Day 11: morning. Pain has settled but the wound feels hot when she changes the bandage. Slight redness around the cut edges. She cleans again, applies more antiseptic.

Day 12: redness has spread ~3cm beyond the wound. **Infection setting in.** No antibiotics. Folk-remedy options: honey (none in tundra), garlic (not available), willow bark (Practiced plant-ID — she can identify, has small amount; mild antibacterial). Uses willow.

Day 13: mild fever. Wound discharge. She's mostly bedridden. Can move around camp slowly with a stick as crutch.

**§14.13 saliency** begins to dampen — fever + pain reduces her perception of camp surroundings. She doesn't notice that her stored food has begun emitting odor that may attract scavengers. Cache placement was rushed during initial setup.

### Beat 07 — Days 14-15, the fox

Day 14: she sees fresh fox tracks at the food cache. Fox didn't get in (cache hung), but it tried. Tracks visible in soft soil. She manages to re-secure the cache slightly better. Burns more calories doing this with the injured leg.

Day 15: wound clearly infected now. Streak of red running ~6cm up the thigh. Fever climbing. **Body cold-tolerance reducing** — illness is compounding cold-management.

**Decisions:**
1. Stay at camp, continue self-treatment, hope it stabilizes
2. Try to walk back to drop-off point (~16km return) for evacuation
3. Use signal flares to call for emergency (limited to 3 flares, no certainty of response)

She chooses 1 — staying. Hopes immune system + willow + rest will turn the tide. She fires no flare yet.

**Body state end of Day 15:**
- Warmth: 60%
- Hunger: 45%
- Tiredness: 70%
- Fever: 38.5°C
- **Pain (wound): ~55% impairment**
- **Systemic infection visible in wound + lymphatic streak**

## Days 16-19 — fever rising, decisions narrowing

### Beat 08 — Days 16-18, deepening fever

Day 16-18 blur in fever-state. Tova manages basic survival: tends fire, melts water, eats sparingly. She lights one flare on Day 17 evening as a last-shot attempt — visible from afar but no response (she's far from any active patrol route).

**§14.13 saliency** is significantly degraded. She misses signs of weather changing.

By Day 18 evening: temperature has dropped to -12°C (winter pushing in). Her camp fire needs more fuel than her depleted reserves can supply. She has to drag deadfall further than usual on a wounded leg.

### Beat 09 — Day 19, the wolves

Day 19 dawn: she emerges from sleep into ~-15°C cold. Fire dead. She's shivering uncontrollably. Hunger 30%. Fever continues.

**Three wolves at the edge of camp**, ~80m off. They've been drawn by:
- Her diminished fire-maintenance (no recent smoke = less aversion)
- Her stored food cache's scent buildup
- Possibly her own infection scent (illness = vulnerability signal to predators)
- Reduced human-activity radius around camp (her not patrolling as actively)

**Per §13.2 Camp Stalkers + Path C predatory pathway**: this is the early stage of behavioral memory updating. They're investigating, not yet attacking.

She fumbles for bow. Hand-eye coordination compromised by fever. Misses a warning shot at 70m — arrow goes well wide. Sound spooks them briefly but they don't leave.

**She fires another flare** — startles them; they retreat ~200m, watch.

She rebuilds fire. Spends an hour at high effort. **Caloric cost crushing.** Wolves stay at perimeter the rest of the day, observing.

## Days 20-21 — the cascade closes

### Beat 10 — Day 20, the attempt

Tova decides on Day 20 morning to attempt evacuation. Her cabin is incomplete, her wound is septic, her food is running out, predators are gathering. The cascade is closing.

She packs the bare minimum: water, remaining rations (~2 days), medical kit, knife, axe, one flare. Leaves the heavy gear at camp. Bow she can barely draw.

Sets out toward the drop-off point — ~16km of moderately rough tundra terrain. At her current pace + injury + fever, that's 3-4 days of travel.

**Day 20 covers ~4km.** She's exhausted by mid-afternoon. Hunger drops further. Cold persists. Pain in leg is severe (every step aggravates the wound). She makes rough shelter against a deadfall around 16:00 and sleeps in her bag.

Wolves do not follow that day (their territory ends some kilometers back, she suspects).

### Beat 11 — Day 21, sepsis

Tova wakes pre-dawn Day 21 to **chills and confusion**. Her body is now in septic shock — the infection has spread systemically. Heart rate elevated. Blood pressure dropping. Vision is unfocused.

She tries to get up. Manages to stand. Walks ~50m from her sleep-spot in the wrong direction (saliency severely degraded + fever-confusion). Realizes her error. Tries to correct. Can't quite get back to her pack.

She sits on cold ground. Tries to breathe slowly. Body is failing.

**Around mid-morning Day 21, she dies** of septic shock + secondary exposure. No witness.

The pack she carried sits ~50m off; her sleeping bag and shelter further. The 4km she covered yesterday brought her not quite halfway to evacuation. Even if she'd reached the drop-off point, there's no certainty rescue would have come in time.

## What ended Tova — mechanical reading

The death emerged from these conditions aligning:

1. **Axe slip on Day 10** — initial mechanical event; tool wear + tree-fall mishap; ~6cm deep thigh cut
2. **No antibiotics in kit** — wilderness medical reality; willow-bark folk remedy insufficient against deep-tissue infection
3. **Self-treatment quality grade B-C** — Builder is Practiced in first-aid, not Expert; treatment held briefly but couldn't overcome bacterial load
4. **Stationary recovery in cold weather** — calorie budget for healing + warmth + camp maintenance was negative
5. **Predator pressure rising** — illness scent + reduced fire-discipline + food-cache odor attracted wolves; defensive caloric cost compounded
6. **Late evacuation decision** — she committed to staying too long; by Day 20 she lacked the reserves to make 16km
7. **Septic shock** — infection became systemic; fever + confusion compounded; degraded saliency caused her to walk the wrong direction at the end

No single thing killed her. The cascade did. **Injury-cascade death signature — distinct from Run 01's predator-pounce and Run 02's cold-cascade.** All three are mechanically honest emergent outcomes.
