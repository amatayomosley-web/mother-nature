# Run 10 — Trapper, Forest, Late Autumn (Trapline Crafting Probe)

Date of run: 2026-05-10
Status: Day 27 in-game, character died (predatory bear at camp, attracted by accumulated catch-scent across trapline)
Run author: William + Claude (design exercise)

## Setup

- **Character**: "Heath" (placeholder name) — Trapper node, early-30s, multi-generation trapping family
- **Biome**: Forest, late autumn (October equivalent — leaves down or coming down; ground still soft from last week's rain; first frost expected within days)
- **Season**: Pelts at peak (fur grown in for winter); animals fattening; scent disperses fast on cool dry air; bears in hyperphagic pre-hibernation push (per §4.6 Autumn)
- **Time at start**: Mid-morning Day 1
- **Scenario**: 30-day expedition to build and run a working trapline of 20+ sets across multiple ecotones. Established camp at edge of an open meadow that backs onto deep woods, transitioning to a creek 200m east. Heath knows this drainage from previous seasons.

### Starting kit

- Trapper standard (per §15.2): snares + spear + knife
- Wire: 10m steel snare-gauge (~16ga), 5m heavier foothold-gauge (~12ga)
- Tools: knife, hatchet, multi-tool
- Scent / preservation: salt 250g, vinegar 250ml
- Fish: 12 mixed hooks
- Handling: cotton gloves (neutral scent, body-handling), rubberized spare gloves (for cold-water / butchery work)
- Bait box (sealed — vinyl-lined, scent-isolated)
- Notebook + pencil (paper, no digital — this is a diegetic compendium-adjacent tool, not a HUD)
- Bedroll, oiled tarp, 10 days preserved food, fire kit
- Scent-neutralizer kit: charcoal (powdered), pine needles (fresh-gathered on site), local mud
- (No bow. Heath is Trapper-primary, not Hunter — Run 01's lesson is that the Trapper's tooling answer to "wounded large game" is NOT a bow; it's never wound large game in the first place.)

### Compendium grants at start

- Expert: snares (neck/foot variants), foothold sets, deadfall family (figure-4, Paiute, Samson), trap placement on game trails, choke-point reading, scent-management, processing trapped catches, rabbit / hare / fox / mink / marten / muskrat
- Practiced: cordage and shaping (Forester strong-secondary), bait plants and scent-neutralizers (Forager mid), survivalist baseline (fire, shelter, navigation)
- Novice: predator-reading (Trapper Novice on bear / cougar / wolf — the cost of node specialization is that Heath reads trail signs as game first, predator second)
- Glimpsed: weather forecasting beyond 6-hour, distant fishing technique

### Skill node

Trapper — snare-craft, foothold-craft, deadfall-craft, trapline-routing, processing, scent-management, set-quality grading

### Goal

Stand up a 20+ set trapline across multiple ecotones, run it daily, surface what makes traplines work and fail in MN. No predetermined end-state.

## Days 1-2 — site survey + first sets (Week 1 set-out)

### Beat 01 — Day 1, scouting the ecotones

Heath spends Day 1 walking, not setting. Three ecotones within 800m: meadow (rabbit, hare, ground squirrel), deep wood west (marten, fox, fisher), creek bottom east (mink, muskrat). The transition strips between them are where trails concentrate.

**§14.13 saliency at Trapper Expert** fires three categories of detail his Hunter equivalent would miss:

- **Trail-aging.** Stem-bends through tall grass darken as cell rupture and oxidation progress. A 12-hour trail reads graphite-toned; a 3-hour trail reads green. Same pixels, different saliency. Novice sees "a path."
- **Pinch points by tree-spacing.** Two pines 80cm apart on a deer trail aren't "two pines" — they're a forced-narrow a snare can rest at the apex of. Heath catches it; Novice walks past.
- **Scent-marker shrubs.** A juniper at the meadow-wood edge has faint fox urine residue + bark-rub from repeated mark-and-recheck. He maps it as a fox cycle-route.

**Systems**: §14.13 saliency · §13.3 Tracks substrate + age · §5.3 behavioral states (he's reading the *intent* of each track, not just prints)

**Gaps**: T1 per-stem trail-age rendering · T2 scent-marker shrubs as world entities · T3 pinch-point geometry as queryable tile-pair property

### Beat 02 — Day 1 afternoon, the build prep

Back at camp 13:00. Before sets go out, Heath preps materials.

Boils snare wire in tannin tea (oak bark, 90 min). Wire goes from bright steel to dull blue-brown — masks factory oil scent, reads as twig-shadow. **§14.3 multi-axis grading**: this single prep step shifts ALL his snares one grade up — wire-prep quality is global across the trapline. Smokes wire over green pine needles afterward.

**Glove protocol locked**: cotton (neutral) for wire handling, rubberized for catches and meat — never for set-finishing. Mud rub on bare hands for the last 10 seconds of every set (the human-scent strip). Muscle memory, not a UI prompt.

**Gaps**: T4 wire-prep as batch quality modifier · T5 glove state tracking

### Beat 03 — Day 2, the first six sets (cluster A — meadow rabbit run)

Heath walks to a rabbit-run flagged yesterday: three trails converging at a thicket gap.

He sets **six snares in a staggered line** — three live today, three positions flagged with a pebble-marker but unset. He'll add those in 5-6 days so the scent ages across the cluster instead of saturating at once. **Scent-stagger**: multi-set clusters increase catch rate but broadcast "human worked here recently" if installed at once.

Per-set protocol:
1. Cotton gloves on. Wire shaped to loop, anchor to a green sapling (spring-pole bottleneck) or static brush.
2. Loop sized to rabbit neck (~12cm, 10cm off ground).
3. Gloves off, mud rub on bare hand. Adjust loop angle barehanded — last 10 seconds are scent-critical.
4. Cotton back on, walk out. Brush retreat with a pine bough — not erasing tracks, breaking the boot-press into a non-straight line.

**§14.3 grading**: trigger ~1.2kg, weather-resilience ~3 weeks dry, age-to-degradation ~14-20 days at this build. All six Day-2 sets log Grade A. **§14 mastery delta**: a Novice trapper at the same trail would set the loop too high (mental-modeling hare not rabbit), anchor on dead sapling, handle bare-handed on the finishing touch. Their catch rate would be ~1/5 of Heath's. Some catches would be live-injured rabbits suffering for hours — the kill-vs-hold ratio on a badly-set snare is moral cost as well as catch cost.

**Gaps**: T6 snare multi-axis grading · T7 scent-stagger cluster mechanic · T8 anchor-type fitness · T9 kill-vs-hold ratio (§15 corner — trap holds, animal isn't dead, player arrives at a suffering captive)

## Days 3-7 — building the trapline (Week 1 set-out continued)

### Beat 04 — Day 3, deep-wood line (cluster B — marten / fisher)

1.2 km west, marten sign on a fallen-log corridor along a low ridge — martens travel logs because small-mammal scent reads as food trail to them.

**Three Samson deadfalls** along the log corridor (toggle-and-bait; toggle release drops flat weight; clean spine-kill on marten). Build time ~25 min Expert; Novice ~75 min with wrong bait-stick angle. Bait: pine-nut paste from his trail mix + flake of dried meat in the toggle notch.

**Expert reading: which logs are actively used.** Three of five candidate logs show clean upper surface (paws scuff lichen), no spider-web spanning crawl direction (broken by recent passage), faint musk-marker at log's down-end. He sets on those three, ignores the other two. Novice would set on all five and waste two deadfalls.

Sets 7-9 done. Bait handled with rubberized gloves to keep human-scent off bait surface.

### Beat 05 — Day 4, creek bottom (cluster C — mink / muskrat)

200m east to creek. Mink scat on a half-submerged log; muskrat-runs into the bank.

- **Two #1 footholds** with heavier-wire anchor-cable. Sets 10, 11.
- **One basket trap** woven on site from green willow (~2h — Forester-secondary efficiency), baited with hook + dried meat, anchored mid-creek with a cobble. Set 12.

**§1 No Dominant Strategy surfaces**: each trap class trades differently. Footholds kill rarely (muskrat may drown if anchor pulls deep; otherwise hold-alive, dispatch required). Basket kills nothing — everything caught is alive at check. Snare (cluster A) kills fast or slow depending on neck-position. NO TRAP TYPE IS DOMINANT — each fits a target and check-cadence differently.

### Beat 06 — Days 5-7, the rest of the line (clusters D, E, F)

Day 5 cluster D: **three figure-4 deadfalls** on a stump-cluster squirrels are using as a mast-cache. Sets 13-15. Figure-4 is the universal small-game deadfall: three sticks, flat rock, baited.

Day 6 cluster E: **pit trap** on a sandy rabbit run, ~50cm deep, lattice cover, baited rim. 4h digging — labor-intensive but re-catches. Set 16. Plus **spring-pole snare** at a brushy fence-crossing. Set 17.

Day 7 cluster F: **Paiute deadfall** for porcupine. Set 18. Plus **trotline** in creek pool downstream of cluster C — six hooks bank-to-bank, baited with grub larvae from rotting wood. Sets 19-24.

**End Day 7**: 24 active sets across 6 clusters. Daily route camp → A → E → D → F → camp → B → C → camp loops 6.5km. Heath alternates halves (A/E/D/F one day, B/C the next) for body-cost management and 48h cluster soak.

Body state end Week 1: warmth 90%, hunger 70%, tiredness 65%. Pelts: 0 (Week 1 is set-out, not harvest). Ration burn ~30% of preserved food.

**Gaps**: T10 build-time scaling (Novice 3-4× Expert) · T11 trapline route as saved object without minimap UI (notebook annotation? pebble-cairn markers?) · T12 cluster-level vs set-level grading · T13 multi-set cross-noise interaction (snared rabbit screams; deadfall'd marten doesn't; cluster A noise profile is different from cluster B, affecting predator attraction)

## Days 8-15 — running the line (Week 2 calibration)

### Beat 07 — Day 8, first checks

A/E/D/F half at dawn. Wind west in his face — he walks UP the line so his scent doesn't precede.

- **Cluster A**: 1 of 6 fired. Rabbit, neck-broken clean, ~6h dead. Quality A. Field-dress on site (15 min Expert). Re-sets with same wire.
- **Cluster E**: pit and spring-pole empty.
- **Cluster D**: figure-4 #1 sprung no-catch (leaf on bait stick). Other two intact, baits eaten by something too small to trigger.
- **Cluster F**: Paiute empty. Trotline: 2 of 6 hooks with small panfish.

Yield Day 8: 1 rabbit + 2 panfish ≈ 700 cal, 1 pelt.

### Beat 08 — Day 9, B/C run + the first hold-alive

Cluster B: 0 of 3. One bait eaten clean no trigger (marten cautious or too small). Re-baits.

Cluster C: **foothold #1 has a mink. ALIVE.** ~3-4h in trap; foot held not broken; animal exhausted fighting anchor.

**§15 dispatch decision fires.** A captive mink is fast and mean.

1. Distance: mink at 60cm anchor; Heath stays at 90cm — out of teeth-range, in reach for kill move.
2. Weapon: spear butt-end (reserves tip for predator self-defense; club to head doesn't damage pelt; spear-tip puncture does).
3. Hit zone: cranium. Vital. Instant if landed clean.
4. Failure mode: glancing strike on moving mink = animal not killed, fight escalates. Even Expert tier isn't 100%. Novice misses cranium 30%+ of the time and beats the animal repeatedly, ruining pelt and prolonging suffering.

Heath lands clean. Mink dead in one. **§15 hit-zone model holds on captives**: lethality is hit-zone, not HP. Expert dispatch is high-probability success because his hand knows where the cranium is, not an automatic.

Pelt intact. Yield: 1 mink, prime pelt, ~600 cal meat (most reserved for bait — mink is less appetizing than rabbit and the bait economy needs it).

Foothold #2 sprung-empty (something stepped in, struggled out, anchor-wire abrasion, no blood). Escape-with-injury — that catch carries a foot-wound for days.

**Gaps**: T14 captive dispatch as §15 hit-zone (cranium target, mastery-modulated) · T15 escape-with-injury outcome · T16 pelt-damage taxonomy by set type

### Beat 09 — Days 10-12, calibration and the zero day

Days 10-11 rhythm: 2 rabbits + 1 squirrel + 1 muskrat (foothold #2 rebound catch) Day 10; 1 grouse on the spring-pole snare Day 11 (unexpected — spring-pole pulled the bird off the ground and broke its neck on the recoil).

**Day 12: zero across the line.** Heath inspects:
- Cluster A: 2 of 3 sprung-empty. Foxes have been biting at the snare-wire — they recognize wire-shape as human-set after multiple days of catch-scent. **§2 mechanical behavior**: this is foxes whose `behavioral-memory.this-wire-shape` weight crossed an avoidance threshold. Not scripted "Day 10 fails"; the cluster's scent + repeated catch-evidence accumulated until per-individual learning fired.
- Cluster D: bait gone from all three, no triggers. Squirrels have learned the figure-4 geometry; they reach in from the side.
- Cluster B: still 0. Marten may have shifted south for winter.

Heath's response (Expert):
1. Pulls cluster A's three live snares, moves to a fresh sub-section 30m down trail (cluster A2). Adds the three staggered positions live — he's abandoning the stagger plan to chase catch rate.
2. Rebuilds cluster D's figure-4s with bait-stick rotated 90°.
3. More aggressive bait on cluster B; if no return in 2 days, will move that cluster east.

**Gaps**: T17 cluster-level predator-learning (`behavioral-memory.this-wire-shape`) · T18 small-game bait-angle adaptation · T19 catch-rate decay curve per cluster age (uncalibrated estimate: catch rate halves every ~5 days without refresh)

### Beat 10 — Days 13-15, the second wind

Cluster A2 catches 4 rabbits over three days — refresh works. Cluster D rotated catches 2 squirrels. Cluster B still 0; Heath pulls it Day 15, scouts 600m east, finds an older martens'-corridor, resets as cluster B2. 24 active sets maintained. Trotline yields 6 panfish across Week 2 — most used as bait. Trotline is a bait engine, not a calorie engine.

**Week 2 total**: 5 rabbits, 1 mink, 1 muskrat, 1 grouse, 3 squirrels, 6 panfish. ~5,200 cal net after bait. 5 prime pelts.

Body end Week 2: warmth 85%, hunger 50%, tiredness 75% (6.5km/day on rough ground).

## Days 16-22 — yield + the scent economy (Week 3 yield)

### Beat 11 — Days 16-18, the harvest peak

Week 3 is take-home week:

- Day 16: 2 rabbits (A2), 1 fox (foothold #1, cluster C — came down to creek, stepped on trap). Fox dispatch: spear-shaft to cranium, rubberized gloves (fox teeth break skin). Pelt prime, ~3,000 cal lean meat. **Cluster C scent magnified**: fox-fear scent + handler scent + dispatch residue. Cluster C is now a scent-magnet.
- Day 17: 1 marten on B2 (clean Samson kill). Prime marten pelt — highest-value market pelt of the trip.
- Day 18: 1 muskrat (basket trap finally produces after 13 days), 2 rabbits (A2), 1 squirrel (D rotated), 1 panfish.

Pelts end Day 18: 9 prime (1 mink, 1 fox, 1 marten, 4 rabbit, 1 muskrat, 1 squirrel). Even if the trip ended now, Week 3 alone has justified the trapline.

### Beat 12 — Day 19, the bear sign

Day 19 morning, walking cluster C → camp. **Bear sign on the creek path.** Adult brown bear, ~36h old by print-edge dryness. Heading deep wood → creek, toward cluster C.

**§14.13 saliency for Trapper Expert** reads tracks correctly at game-sign level: ~600kg adult, walking gait, drifting east toward creek bottom. But Heath's **predator-reading is NOVICE** (Trapper node, per starting grants). What he reads as "bear drifted to creek" is correct-as-far-as-it-goes but misses the load-bearing inference: **a bear drifting along a scent line in pre-hibernation hyperphagia is scent-following.** Cluster C — site of two foothold catches, mink and fox dispatches, 11 days of accumulation — is a strong emitter. The bear has likely been tracking the cluster's scent gradient for ~24h.

The bear's mechanical state (which Heath only partially reads):
- `hunger-state` HIGH (autumn hyperphagia)
- `behavioral-memory.this-creek-bottom` updating "fresh-meat-scent recurring"
- `territory-status` transient (edge of range)
- `personality.curiosity` MEDIUM-HIGH

Notebook entry: "*bear track, creek bottom, 36h, large male, drifting east*." Exactly what a Novice predator-reader writes — accurate, missing inference. Heath's response is Trapper-appropriate but predator-naive:
- Skip cluster C 48h (let it cool)
- NOT pull sets (2 footholds + 1 basket invested)
- NOT move camp (creek-drift reads as away-from-camp gradient)

A Hunter Expert would pull cluster C immediately.

**§1 No Dominant Strategy as a trapline cost**: the Trapper's blindspot is exactly what makes him a Trapper. Expert trap-economics kept him from pulling profitable sets; Novice predator-pressure kept him from doing what a Hunter would.

**Gaps**: T20 cross-node saliency gap (Trapper-Expert reads game-sign at Expert; predator-sign at Novice — same track, two saliency overlays) · T21 trapline scent-emission as apex `behavioral-memory` input — Path C Camp Stalker pathway applied to trapline cluster, not just food cache

### Beat 13 — Days 20-21, the soak

Heath runs A/E/D/F skipping B/C. Yield holds: 3 rabbits, 1 squirrel, 1 grouse, plus 1 more marten on cluster B2 (2 from this corridor now).

Day 21 he stops at the creek edge to refill water, looks toward cluster C — no bear sign on the camp-side bank. Reads the absence as good. Plans cluster C tomorrow.

End Day 21: 11 prime pelts, ~7,500 cal smoked meat hung 4m up the spruce east of camp.

### Beat 14 — Day 22, cluster C destroyed, the carrion chain confirms

Day 22 dawn. Wind east today — scent drifts toward deep wood, he notes but proceeds.

He reaches creek. **Cluster C is destroyed.** Both footholds torn — anchor cables broken, one trap gone, one bent. Basket trap intact but ransacked. Bear scat on the bank, ~12h fresh.

Track inference: bear came overnight, responded to *accumulated scent*, not to any single catch (no catch was in the trap when bear arrived). Tore the traps from anchor with brute force.

**§13.2 Camp Stalker / Carrion Chain pathway active.** Cluster C is now a confirmed scent-feedback loop: bear visits, finds nothing, behavioral-memory updates "this-creek-bottom-yields-traps" but no payoff yet. Next step in the chain is the bear waiting for a catch, OR finding Heath himself at the cluster during a check.

Heath finally reads the chain. Pulls the basket trap. Salvages one foothold. Notebook: "*bear destroyed cluster C, recovered 1 trap, pulled basket. Will not re-set this drainage.*"

Decision tree end Day 22:
1. Move all sets away from creek — accept reduced ecotone coverage
2. Pull entire trapline and move camp — sacrifice Week 4 yield  
3. Continue A2/E/D/F/B2, stay 200m+ from creek

He chooses option 3. Non-creek clusters are productive. Successful Week 3 already banked. He does not move camp.

**Gaps**: T22 bear-on-trapline as Carrion Chain extension (§13.2 currently kill-site-specific; trapline cluster is functionally equivalent at lower scent intensity, longer curve) · T23 trap-destruction by predator (recoverable vs not by trap class) · T24 cluster-scent decay rate when unrefreshed

## Days 23-27 — predator pressure (Week 4)

### Beat 15 — Days 23-25, productive remainder

- Day 23: 2 rabbits (A2), 1 marten (B2, 3rd from corridor), 1 squirrel (D), 1 panfish.
- Day 24: 1 fox at pit trap (E — first pit-fox; dispatch from pit-rim with extended-reach spear-shaft, lower-angle cranium strike, clean). 1 rabbit (A2).
- Day 25: 0 across the line. Wind shifted overnight. Heath scouts outward, finds NEW bear sign 400m north of camp — smaller print, second individual. Two bears now active in drainage, both autumn-hyperphagic.

End Day 25: warmth 80%, hunger 50%, tiredness 80% (24 days of 6.5km/day). 14 prime pelts. ~9,000 cal smoked meat banked.

### Beat 16 — Day 26, the alert

Day 26 dawn. Heath stepping out of camp toward cluster A2.

**He sees a bear at his food cache.** Cache 4m up the spruce east of camp. The bear — the larger original from cluster C, by track ID — at the base of the tree, looking up. Hasn't climbed (brown bears climb less readily than black bears, 4m is at the edge of interest). Evaluating.

Heath ~60m off, downwind. Bear hasn't seen him. **The bear's been tracking the carrion chain** — first cluster C scent, now camp's smoked-meat cache. The chain has tightened by ~200m.

Three real-time options:
1. Retreat, accept cache loss
2. Scare with noise (works for some bears, may trigger defensive-aggression in others)
3. Stand at distance, let bear evaluate and drift

Heath chooses 3 — safest, and his Trapper-Novice predator handling lacks the Hunter muscle memory for 2. Stays at 60m downwind behind a pine. Bear stands at cache ~4 minutes, doesn't climb, drifts west out of sight at 90m.

**§5.3 behavioral state held: investigatory, not predatory** — bear is in scent-follow / cache-evaluate, not hunting Heath yet. But each repetition updates `this-area-has-food`, `this-cache-shape-is-takeable`, `this-human-is-near`.

Heath moves the smoked-meat cache: 800m northeast, different tree, 5m up. Two trips on tired legs. Packs trap cache (recovered foothold, spare wire, salt, vinegar) ready for bug-out tomorrow.

**Gaps**: T25 camp-cache scent emission accumulates per time-hung (25-day cache emits more strongly than fresh, separate from quantity) · T26 camp-cache + trapline-cluster scent gradient overlap creating regional attractor

### Beat 17 — Day 27, what ends Heath

Day 27 dawn. Heath wakes to the sound of his pack rustling. **The bear is in camp.**

The moved cache reduced one signal, but Heath's sleeping body is now the strongest signal in the area, and the bear's behavioral memory has rolled `investigate` → `approach-human-area`. **§13.2 Camp Stalker pathway matured.**

The bear is ~10m from the bedroll, head down, sniffing Heath's pack. Heath's spear leans against the tarp pole 3m off. Knife in belt. He's lying inside the bedroll.

Per **bear-ai Path D** (Surprise <10m) AND **Path C** (Camp Stalker matured), the bear's defensive-attack weight and predatory-attack weight are both elevated. Personality variance matters: this bear's `aggression-baseline` is mid-high — he's the one who tore cluster C apart for empty traps. Low-aggression bear wouldn't invest that effort.

Heath moves slowly out of the bedroll, reaching for the spear. The bear's head comes up. **Eye contact at <10m with a hyperphagic apex predator.** Utility AI re-evaluates: target-visible, target-low-posture, target-reaching-for-weapon, target-isolated. **Predatory-attack weight crosses threshold.**

The bear charges.

Heath raises the spear. Trapper-primary, spear-handling at competent-baseline, not Hunter-Expert. Spear positioned wrong: butt against ground (right stance), but tip not steady. Bear hits tip with upper chest. Tip penetrates ~12cm into shoulder muscle — not vital. Mass + momentum drives Heath backward; spear shaft breaks.

Bear on him. Bite to neck. **§15 hit-zone: vital, fatal.** Unconscious ~5s, dead ~30s.

The wounded bear retreats ~80m and beds. Survives the shoulder wound. Remembers this campsite. **Now a confirmed Path C Camp Stalker for any future human in this drainage.**

### Beat 18 — Aftermath

**§13.2 Carrion Chain activates on a human body**: ravens within hours, coyotes within a day, the wounded bear returning within 48h once shoulder-pain subsides. By Day 30 the bear has fed on Heath and updated `human-area-yields-food`.

Heath's notebook survives (paper, in the tarp pocket). His 14 pelts hang at the moved cache, undisturbed — the bear didn't find them; the 800m offset worked, just too late for Heath himself.

His player account retains the Field Notes entry that will unlock per §14.12 — death-triggered ethology entry "the brown bear in autumn" or similar, describing mechanisms (hyperphagia, scent-following, behavioral-memory accumulation, camp investigation pathway). Available to his next character. The trapline he built will still be set when the next character walks past it — silent infrastructure outliving its maker, until someone re-sets it or it ages out.

## What ended Heath — mechanical reading

The death emerged from these conditions aligning:

1. **Trapper-primary node** — Expert at trap-craft, Novice at predator-reading. The cross-node saliency gap (T20) meant his Day-19 bear-track reading was *trap-relevant correct* and *predator-relevant under-reactive*.
2. **Cluster C scent accumulation** — 11 days of dispatched-catch scent (mink, fox dispatches + handler scent + bait residue) created a regional attractor (T21 surfaced this as a load-bearing mechanic).
3. **Pre-hibernation hyperphagic bear** — autumn timing meant the bear's hunger-state weight was elevated baseline (§4.6); a summer bear would have under-pursued the scent.
4. **Heath's Trapper-appropriate but predator-naive response Day 22** — pulling cluster C alone wasn't enough; the camp itself was already in the bear's gradient.
5. **Camp-cache scent emission** — 9,000 cal of smoked meat, hung at 4m, emitting for weeks (T25). Heath's cache-relocation Day 26 was correct, but the relocation came after the bear had already mapped the camp area.
6. **Heath's own sleeping-body scent** — the strongest single signal once the cache moved; a hyperphagic bear in confirmed approach mode will check the spot the human sleeps.
7. **Spear-vs-charge at <10m** — Trapper has spear in starting kit but lacks Hunter-Expert brace-stance; the spear-tip penetrated muscle, not chest cavity. The weapon was right; the technique was Trapper-tier, not Hunter-tier.
8. **No bow** — Heath chose Trapper-primary loadout; the Trapper's lethality answer to bears is *never let it get to bears in the first place*, which fails when the trapline IS what brings the bear.

No single thing killed him. The cascade did — and the cascade was specifically the **trapline cascade**: clusters → accumulated scent → carrion-chain analog at lower intensity → apex behavioral-memory update → camp investigation → predatory attack. **This is what §13.2 Camp Stalker looks like when the player's PROFESSION is the scent emitter.**

Distinct death signature: **trapline-scent-driven predator escalation.** Different from Run 01 (cougar-pounce stalking accumulation), Run 02 (cold-cascade), Run 03 (injury-infection cascade), Run 04 (prey-defensive-attack during rut). Five death signatures across five runs; all mechanically honest.

## Architecture stress-test results (Run 10 full)

- **§1 No Dominant Strategy held under sustained pressure.** Trapper-primary was viable 26 days; 14 prime pelts + ~9,000 cal banked is a successful run by any economic measure. The cost matrix held: Trapper has passive-capacity advantage (set + walk away), predator-vulnerability disadvantage (no Hunter ranged dispatch; Novice predator-reading). Rifle Hunter would carry different costs (regional scent + noise; meat damage). Forager would have far lower yield. Each node's trade-off is real and load-bearing.
- **§2 Mechanical Behavior held throughout.** Foxes biting cluster A's wires emerged from `behavioral-memory.this-wire-shape`. The bear's escalation emerged from accumulated scent + hunger + behavioral memory crossing thresholds. Heath's death emerged from utility-AI re-evaluation at <10m with target-low-posture detected.
- **§13.2 Carrion Chains generalized correctly** — trapline cluster is a lower-intensity, longer-accumulation analog of the kill-site carrion chain. Same mechanic class, different timescale (24h kill-site vs 11d cluster). The chain isn't kill-site-specific; it's a scent-emission-driven AI pathway that works on any persistent emitter.
- **§14 mastery delta produced felt differences** across set-quality, build-time, anchor-fitness reads, kill-vs-hold ratio, dispatch success. The Trapper's debuff-removal stack on knot-craft, wire-shaping, choke-point-reading delivered grade-A outputs a Novice could not replicate.
- **§14.13 saliency in Trapper-domain worked.** Trail-aging at per-stem-bend resolution, scent-marker objects, pinch-point geometry all surfaced. **The cross-node saliency gap is the §14 design honoring its own debuff-removal model: saliency is per-domain, not global. Trapper Expert reads game-sign at Expert saliency; predator-sign at Novice saliency. SAME TRACK.**
- **§15 hit-zone dispatch on captives worked**, with mastery-modulated success rate. Mink + fox dispatches landed; bear-defense did not (Trapper-Novice spear-brace vs charging-bear is the wrong technique). The model held.
- **§5 Wildlife agents persistent across the run** — the Day-19 bear was the same agent as the Day-22 cluster-destroyer, the Day-26 cache-investigator, and the Day-27 killer. Apex individual identity (§12.8) made the cascade legible in retrospect: not "a bear" each time, but THIS bear's behavioral memory accumulating.

### Kill-check cadence

- **Dawn check**: optimal for snare/spring-pole (overnight catches; predator scavenging risk still present but lower than full day)
- **Dusk check**: optional secondary; rare-set check; foothold-water-set check (to prevent overnight drowning of caught species you want to keep alive for pelt-only)
- **Midday**: avoid running active line midday — scent broadcasts most strongly in warmest hours
- **Delayed-check failure modes**:
  - 4-8h: animal may escape (chewed-foot from foothold; pulled-from-snare; squirmed-from-basket)
  - 8-24h: predator scavenging (fox claims snared rabbit; coyote claims foothold mink)
  - 24-48h: decomposition begins; pelt damage; meat marginal

### Big takeaways from Run 10

1. **The trapline IS a scent system.** Every cluster is a sustained emitter. §13.2 was designed around kill sites, but it generalizes immediately to any persistent emitter — clusters, caches, butchery sites, a sleeping spot held too long. **§13.2 should be reframed as the SCENT-PERSISTENCE chain, not the kill-site-specific chain.**
2. **Trapper-primary is mechanically distinct from Hunter and viable.** 26 days, 14 pelts, 9,000 cal, 24 sets concurrent. Distinct death-pathway too: trapper deaths come from scent-saturation cascades, not stalk-encounters. Different death signature confirms No Dominant Strategy at the run level.
3. **Cross-node saliency gap (T20) is the §14.13 trapper-specific contribution.** Saliency isn't global; it's per-domain. The Trapper sees trail-as-game-sign at Expert resolution and trail-as-predator-sign at Novice resolution. SAME TRACK. The cost of specialization is the inference layer the other nodes carry.
4. **Scent-stagger broke down under catch-rate pressure (Day 13).** The cluster scent-management plan was abandoned when Heath chased yield. Scent-stagger discipline is a learned skill that catch-pressure undermines — real-world trappers face this exact tension.
5. **§15 hit-zone on captive dispatch is load-bearing for the trapper experience.** A trapper makes more §15 decisions per run than a hunter — every foothold, every alive-snared animal, every live-fish. Model held; T14 needs spec.
6. **The bear's behavioral memory accumulated across 9 days before lethal payoff.** Day 19 (bear sign at creek) → Day 22 (cluster C destruction) → Day 26 (cache investigation) → Day 27 (predatory attack at <10m). Four touch-points. **The death was not random; the cascade was a week of warning, none of it labeled by the system. This is the No-UI-alert design honoring itself.**
7. **Hunting vs Trapping trade-off resolves cleanly.** Hunting wins when ranged shot opportunity exists; calorie-per-hour matters more than infrastructure; mobility matters. Trapping wins when passive capacity matters; mobility cost is high (Heath ran 6.5km/day on his line); pelt economics matter (target species differ from Hunter big-game). **Late autumn favors trapping — and favors apex predators in pre-hibernation push simultaneously.** The trapper's economic window and the bear's hunger window overlap exactly.
8. **Predator escalation cascade ended Heath. The mechanics supported it without scripting.** §1 (trapline density paid for in predator attraction), §2 (each step from inputs aligning), §13.2 (Camp Stalker Path C threshold crossed), §14 (Trapper-Novice predator-reading load-bearing in the death), §15 (hit-zone on spear-vs-charge), bear-ai.md (Path C + Path D fired). The architecture composed correctly.
