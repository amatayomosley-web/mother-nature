# Run 16 — Forager (Wild-Tender), Forest, Late-Spring-to-Early-Autumn, Managed-Wild Exploration

Date of run: 2026-05-10
Status: 90-day exploratory simulation; character alive at Day 90; outcome durable (notebook recovered, patches stewarded)
Run author: William + Claude (design exercise)

> **ALTERNATIVE-TO-FARMING CAVEAT.** This run sits next to Run 13 (Ren, forest kitchen garden) and Run 14 (Vasco, desert irrigation). Run 13 asked *can a Forager farm*. Run 14 asked *can a Builder irrigate a desert plot*. This run asks the **third-way question**: between pure foraging (no intervention, take what the wild offers) and farming (clear land, plant domesticates, defend against wildlife/weather/pest pressure), is there a stewardship-of-wild-patches lane that earns its keep? The Mother Nature design has no committed system for this — §14 Forager has no Wild-Tender sub-node, §13 has no patch-state model, §14.13 has no patch-state-over-time saliency. Treat magnitudes (yields, patch counts, labor hours, rotation cycles) as proposals to surface the shape of the problem, not commitments. The run's purpose is to ask whether Wild-Tender is a real sub-node, or whether it collapses into Forager-Expert.

## Phase 1 — Days 1-14 — May patches, ramp emergence, competition clearing

### Beat 01 — Day 1, the territory walk

Iri does not unpack. She walks. Her first day is a re-acquaintance circuit of the closest five patches — sunchoke field (patch 1), ramp grove (patch 5), sorrel meadow (patch 11), sunflower burn-scar edge (patch 14), hop-vine glade (patch 10). About 8km of walking, no harvest, notebook out at every stop.

**§14.13 saliency at Wild-Tender-Expert tier surfaces things no other run has framed**:

- At patch 1 (sunchoke), she reads the **patch density relative to her Day-1-prior-year photograph** (mental image, not actual photo). The patch looks ~15% sparser at the southwest corner. Blackberry runners have crossed the edge from patch 12 over the winter. Notebook entry: *"P1 SW corner — blackberry encroach ~2m, mark for thinning; rest of patch unchanged from prior season image."*
- At patch 5 (ramp), she reads the **emergence stage**. The ramps are 4-5cm above ground, leaves still folded vertically — this is pre-peak. **The peak harvest window is the 8-12 day stretch when leaves unfurl flat but bulbs haven't yet committed energy back down**. She's a week early. Notebook: *"P5 emergence Day 1 — peak harvest est. Days 8-12. Do not harvest yet."*
- At patch 11 (sorrel meadow), she reads **woody seedling competition** — three young aspen suckers (~30cm) and a wild rose stem are in the meadow she's been keeping open. Notebook: *"P11 — aspen ×3, rose ×1, cut Day 3-5."*
- At patch 14 (sunflower stand at burn-scar edge), the burn-scar canopy gap is now partially closed by raspberry brake and a young pine. **Sunflower wants direct sun.** Notebook: *"P14 — raspberry encroach south, pine sapling north. Cut Day 4. Sunflower seed not yet scattered for this year; do Day 5-7 after clearing."*
- At patch 10 (hop-vine glade), the standing-dead hop-pole tree (the one she girdled 3 years ago) is still vertical, bark sloughing — good. Hop is just emerging at the base. Notebook: *"P10 — hop emerging Day 1. Pole sound. No competition issues."*

**This saliency is patch-state-over-time, not patch-state-now.** The novice forager looking at the same five patches sees five separate "plants" stands. Iri sees *change-against-prior-state* at each. **This is the central proposal of the Wild-Tender sub-node**: §14.13 saliency operates not just on current entity properties (Bo's umbel-stem-character, Eira's wood-grain-spiral) but on **delta-against-remembered-state**. The notebook is the diegetic surface that makes this differential read possible.

**§14.13 extension proposed**: saliency-over-time. Bo's saliency reads the cougar in the brush *right now*. Iri's saliency reads the patch *against its prior selves*. **This is a distinct mode of the same shader system, riding on patch-state-persistent world-data and on player-readable notes/notebook content.**

**Systems invoked**: §14.13 saliency (delta-mode, proposed) · §14.6 hybrid perception (notebook as the labeled back-room equivalent) · long-arc character continuity (4 prior seasons)

**Gaps surfaced**:
- **MW1** — Patch-state as persistent per-shard world data (density, competition load, last-harvest date, last-seed-scatter date, fallow status, marker state). Currently §3 forest biome has no patch-state model — wild stands are ambient terrain, not persistent objects.
- **MW2** — Saliency-over-time as a §14.13 mode: shader reads delta against player-recorded prior state, not just current entity properties.
- **MW3** — Notebook as kit item with persistent data payload; load-bearing for Wild-Tender's saliency mode; vulnerable to loss/destruction.

### Beat 02 — Days 2-3, base camp + first competition cuts

Iri sets a low base camp at the creek-margin between patch 5 (ramp grove) and patch 12 (raspberry brake), 80m from water. Tarp + bough bed + small fire ring. She's been here before, and the previous-season cookfire pit is still visible — she clears the rain-washed ash and reuses the stone ring. **Returning to her own prior site is part of the run's texture**; this is not a first-arrival.

Day 3 morning, patch 11 (sorrel meadow): she cuts the three aspen suckers and the wild rose stem at ground level with her knife, leaves the cuts in the meadow as mulch. ~45 min work. Notebook: *"P11 — aspen + rose cleared D3. Meadow open. Sorrel emerging at expected density."*

Day 3 afternoon, patch 1 (sunchoke field) SW corner: she pulls the blackberry runners by hand-following each runner back to its parent crown in patch 12, then cutting it at the parent crown side — not at the runner tip. **The cut location matters**: cutting at the runner tip lets the parent re-send; cutting at the parent forces the parent to redirect to a different runner. **This is a Wild-Tender competence point**. A pure-Forager-Expert without Wild-Tender experience would cut the runner where they found it, which Iri's prior-year notes record as ineffective. ~90 min work.

Notebook: *"P1 SW — blackberry runners traced back to P12 parent crowns. Cut at parent ends ×6. Watch for re-send Days 12-15."*

**§14.13 Wild-Tender saliency at competition-vs-companion reading**: the same shader that helps Bo distinguish hemlock from cow parsnip on stem-character now helps Iri distinguish "blackberry runner I should cut at parent" from "blackberry runner that's the structural backbone of patch 12 and must NOT be cut." Both look identical to a Novice. Iri's eye reads *which runner is feeding which crown*. The saliency renders the parent-runner more vividly when she's standing in the SW corner with cutting intent.

**This is the §14.13 saliency extension Run 09's Eira surfaced (negative-selection) reframed for Wild-Tender**: Eira rejected three trees in seconds; Iri rejects three runners in seconds, with positive-selection on the one she should cut.

**Gaps surfaced**:
- **MW4** — Competition-vs-companion reading as a Wild-Tender saliency tier (parallel to Eira's negative-selection bowyer-eye and Bo's look-alike discrimination)
- **MW5** — Runner-cut location as a Wild-Tender competence point (cutting at parent vs runner-tip produces observably different re-send behavior — this is the §14 quality-grade axis applied to a tending action)

### Beat 03 — Days 4-7, sunflower bed prep + ramp emergence countdown

Day 4 patch 14 (sunflower burn-scar): she clears the south-side raspberry encroach (~2.5m × 0.8m strip) and cuts the north-side pine sapling at ground level. ~3 hours. Notebook: *"P14 cleared. Patch now open to full sun ~6-8 hrs."*

Day 5 patch 14: she scatters sunflower seed she saved from last year's best head (kept in a wax-paper packet in her pack). Not a row — a **scattered broadcast across the cleared south strip**, mimicking the natural seed-rain pattern. She presses seed lightly into the soil with her trowel back, walks once over the strip to firm. ~20 min work.

**This is the central mechanical difference from Ren's farming in Run 13**: Iri does not till, does not amend soil, does not water (it's mid-spring forest; ground holds moisture), does not fence (the burn-scar opening is not high-edibility-signal to deer mid-spring; sunflower seedlings are not deer's preferred browse in this season). **The labor is ~20 minutes for a patch Ren would have spent ~4 days clearing + amending + bedding + fencing**. The yield will be lower — but the labor input is also vastly lower. **This is the §1 lane proposal in numerical form.**

Days 6-7 patch 5 (ramp grove): she watches. The ramps are at Day 6-7 of unfurling. **She marks 4 individuals with red ribbon — the largest leaves, the densest centers — for seed-save.** These will not be harvested this year. She'll come back at seed-set in late summer to collect the seed for a future scatter at a new ramp site.

Notebook: *"P5 ramp peak imminent. 4 red-ribboned for seed. Harvest opens Day 8-9."*

**Gaps surfaced**:
- **MW6** — Seed-scatter as Wild-Tender process (clear, scatter, press, walk-firm — no till, no amendment); cost is a few minutes; yield is annual; success rate depends on site selection
- **MW7** — Ribbon-marking as a player-emitted world-state object (similar to §13.1 Forest Signs but at-patch-individual resolution, not at-trail-template)
- **MW8** — Seed-save discipline: marking high-yield individuals NOT to harvest, returning at seed-set for collection. Multi-month return-pattern is its own scheduling layer.

### Beat 04 — Days 8-12, ramp harvest (the 1/4 rule in action)

Day 8 morning, patch 5: Iri begins harvest. **Her rule is 1/4 of the patch, never more.** She kneels in a corner of the patch, looks at the dense cluster of unfurled ramp leaves, and starts counting.

The patch holds maybe ~400 visible ramp clumps (each clump is 3-5 bulbs at the soil level, sending up 2-3 leaves). **One quarter is 100 clumps.** She harvests carefully, by clump, with her hand trowel: lift the clump, take 2-3 bulbs, **leave 1-2 in the soil**. The leaves she pinches off above the bulb so the bulb can re-leaf or store for next year. **She does not pull leaves where she's not harvesting bulbs.** Wasted leaves are wasted patch-future.

~4 hours of work over the day. Yield: ~120 ramp bulbs + ~80 leaves, ~600g total fresh weight, ~400 calories worth (ramp is low-density nutritionally; the value is flavor and preservation as a salt-substitute / aromatic for fish + meats she may acquire over the run).

Notebook: *"P5 harvest D8 — 105 clumps harvested out of est. 400 (26%). Acceptable. Patch density visually thinned but core intact. P6 and P7 not touched this year — fallow per rotation."*

Days 9-12: Iri returns to patch 5 in short morning visits to harvest the remaining ~25 clumps she budgeted (broken across days to manage the perishability of fresh ramp), then begins preservation: ramp bulbs cleaned, half preserved in vinegar (small bottle she brought — Bo-style botanist kit pattern), half braided onto cordage and hung in the tarp eave to dry.

**This is the labor profile of Wild-Tender harvest**: short bursts, small per-day yields, careful preservation. Not the all-day all-or-nothing harvest Ren's garden produces.

**Gaps surfaced**:
- **MW9** — Patch-density read as a saliency feature (Iri reads "400 clumps" and "26% harvested" by eye, at Wild-Tender Expert; Novice would read "a lot of ramps" and either over-harvest or under-harvest)
- **MW10** — 1/4 rule (and 1/3 for tubers) as Wild-Tender discipline; the *rule* is a player-knowledge artifact, but the *patch consequence of breaking it* must be modelled — over-harvest year-N reduces patch yield year-N+1, year-N+2, multi-year recovery curve
- **MW11** — Preservation immediately at harvest (vinegar pickle + dry-braid) as Wild-Tender × Forester-cross-craft, similar to Bo's pantry but stretched across the season

### Beat 05 — Days 13-14, sunchoke check + early raspberry sign

Day 13, patch 1 (sunchoke field): Iri inspects emergence. Sunchoke is later than ramp — emerging now, leaves just opening. She measures patch density relative to last year (notebook records "Day 13 prior year: shoots ~25cm; this year ~18cm — cooler spring?"). Patch is healthy but a touch slower. She marks 6 individuals (red ribbon) — the most vigorous early-emerging stalks — for seed-tuber selection in the fall.

Notebook: *"P1 D13 — emergence ~18cm vs prior-year 25cm. Cooler spring tracking 5-7 days behind. 6 individuals red-ribboned for fall tuber save."*

Day 14, patch 12 (raspberry brake south side, the year-A harvest side): canes are leafing out, no flower yet. She walks the patch noting cane density, prunes 3 dead-cane skeletons (clipped at ground level with her knife — dead canes harbour pest fungi), leaves the cuts as mulch. Notebook: *"P12 south — 3 dead canes cut. Live canes healthy. Flower est. Day 25-30."*

End of Day 14: Iri has harvested ~600g ramp (~400 cal stored), prep'd patches 11, 14, 1, 12 for the season ahead, scattered sunflower seed at P14, and walked her territory enough that her notebook holds a current-year baseline she can compare against future visits.

**Body state Day 14**: warmth 90%, hunger 70%, thirst 80%, tiredness 35%. **She is well below the labor stress of any Ren-or-Vasco day in Run 13/14**. The Wild-Tender labor pattern is *short bursts across many patches*, not *long sustained labor on one site*. This is a real difference.

**Calories balance**: forage and ramp harvest = ~400 cal/day, plus mushroom hunts (a small flush of morels she found near P16's deadfall cluster), plus a small grouse she killed by lucky throw of a rock (not snared — opportunistic). She is net-positive on calories. She has eaten down only ~2 days of her 14 days preserved food.

## Phase 2 — Days 15-35 — June patches, raspberry-and-hop, mid-season tending

### Beat 06 — Days 15-22, hop pole check + first raspberry-flower

Day 15-16, patch 10/15 (hop-vine glade + hop-pole tree): hop bines are now ~2m up the standing-dead pole. They want more height to flower. Iri uses her hatchet to top a small live alder ~3m off the hop-pole, then leans the topped trunk to the pole as an additional support — a quick lash with cordage and the hop has more climbing surface. ~90 min work.

Notebook: *"P10 — hop bines 2m on pole D15. Added alder lean-pole. Watch flower Day 35-45."*

Days 17-22 are quiet tending days. Iri's daily rhythm settles:

- **Dawn forage walk** (1-1.5 hr) — mushrooms (this year's flush is moderate), wild greens (sorrel, plantain, dandelion), any patches en route
- **Mid-morning patch visit** (1-2 hr per patch, 1-3 patches per day depending on distance) — check, mark, occasionally harvest small quantity, log
- **Afternoon preservation + camp** (1-1.5 hr) — drying rack tending, ramp braids checked, foraged greens processed
- **Evening notebook** (~30 min) — review day's notes, sketch patch states, plan tomorrow

**This is a fundamentally different daily shape from Ren's farming in Run 13**. Ren had 2.5-3 hr/day fixed on watering and weeding plus the same forage walk. Iri's patch visits are *replacements* for the forage walk, not additions — she's foraging the patches, on a schedule. **The work IS the forage. The forage IS the tending.**

Day 21, patch 12 south (raspberry): first flowers open. Notebook: *"P12 south flower D21. Berry est. Day 38-45."*

### Beat 07 — Days 23-28, sunchoke and patch-2 rotation visit + the snake near-miss

Day 23, patch 2 (sunchoke, year-B in rotation — NOT to be harvested this year, fallow): Iri visits anyway, on principle. **Fallow patches need visits.** She checks for invasive incursion (none significant), notes density (~10% denser than last year — fallow is working), pulls one volunteer raspberry sucker that has crossed from patch 12. ~45 min work, zero harvest.

Notebook: *"P2 fallow — density +10% est. Working. One raspberry sucker pulled."*

This is the Wild-Tender rotation discipline. **A 1/4-rule patch needs a fallow year periodically; a 4-patch sunchoke field rotates so any single patch is hard-harvested once every 4 years.** The notebook tracks which year is which. Lose the notebook, lose the rotation schedule, lose 4 years of yield calibration.

Day 24, patch 5 (ramp grove fallow side — patches 6, 7): she visits both, checks for blackberry encroach (none), confirms density is intact. Quick visit.

Day 26 — **the snake.** Iri is walking the meadow between patches 11 and 14 mid-day, sun on her left, eyes on the ground for the path. She steps over a fallen log. Below the log, on the warm sunny side, a **timber rattlesnake-equivalent** is coiled, basking. She does not see it until she is already past — it does not strike; she is downwind of its threat-trigger and her step did not land within its strike radius. She freezes mid-stride, looks back, sees it, backs up slowly.

**§14.13 saliency at Survivalist-baseline for Iri** is Novice-tier on snake-reading. **A Survivalist-Expert (Run 02-class) would have read the basking-substrate cues — flat sun-warmed rock, fallen-log overhang, mid-morning warm — and approached the log differently.** Iri did not. Mechanically, she was lucky. The same step on a different day with a different snake would have struck her calf, and at her Forager-Practiced medicinal kit, snake-bite is NOT in her treatment scope. **This is one of three "what could have ended Iri" near-misses; the others below.**

Notebook (evening, hand still mildly shaky): *"D26 — timber rattler under log between P11-P14 meadow path. No bite, no strike. Mark log location — DO NOT step over without looking ahead next time. Survivalist-Expert saliency gap noted."*

**Gap surfaced**:
- **MW12** — Forager-Wild-Tender has high saliency in her domain (patch-state) but baseline-Novice saliency in cross-domain hazards (snake, ground-wasp, fall-injury, allergic reaction); the multi-day territorial routine produces compounding exposure to non-Forager hazards that her skill stack does not cover

### Beat 08 — Days 29-35, raspberry first-flush + hop flower + log-cluster mushroom check

Days 29-33, patch 12 south side: raspberry first-flush. Iri's 1/4 rule applies here too — she takes ~25% of ripe berries per visit, walks the patch every other day across the flush window, accumulates ~2kg of fresh berries over 5 days. About half eaten fresh (~700 cal), half preserved (sun-dried into leather-strips on the drying rack she built Day 4 from saplings, parallel to Bo's Run 11 build).

Notebook: *"P12 south flush D29-D33. ~2kg fresh harvest, est. 25% of patch yield. Patch healthy; canes carrying load. North side (P13) untouched, fallow this year — will see in 2027."*

Day 34, patch 10 (hop): bines now ~4m up the pole + alder-lean, just starting to flower. Hop flowers are the harvest target (cones — for tea, for preservation, for trade). She marks: *"P10 D34 — first flower visible. Harvest est. Day 50-60."*

Day 35, patch 16 (inoculated log-cluster mushroom): she visits with her hand trowel and a damp cloth. The log cluster is the project she's been most uncertain about — she transplanted spawn from a productive natural deadfall 3 years ago, and is in year-3 of waiting for first flush. **Today, on the underside of the most-shaded log, she finds three small oyster-mushroom flushes** — pale, young, the first fruiting of her inoculation work.

Notebook: *"P16 D35 — FIRST FLUSH. Three oyster fruits visible. One harvested. Two left for spore. Inoculation worked. Project complete; patch now self-sustaining if logs not disturbed."*

**This is a 3-year-deferred yield landing.** Iri's inoculation effort 3 years ago — a quick 20-minute transplant — now produces a patch she'll harvest annually. **The Wild-Tender labor profile is "small effort, deferred yield, multi-year recurrence."** Compare Ren's garden: weeks of effort, same-season yield, full re-clear next year. **The economics are fundamentally different.**

**Gaps surfaced**:
- **MW13** — Mushroom-log inoculation as Wild-Tender × Forester cross-craft; small per-event labor, multi-year deferred yield, perpetual annual return once established

Body state Day 35: warmth 85%, hunger 75% (well-fed now — berry flush + ramp + foraged greens + mushroom hits), thirst 80%, tiredness 30%. **She is in the best body state of any 35-day mark across all simulated runs.** This is the Wild-Tender labor profile producing the body-state benefit it's supposed to.

## Phase 3 — Days 36-60 — July, sunchoke flowering, peak summer tending

### Beat 09 — Days 36-44, sunchoke flowering + competition adjacent

Day 36-37, patch 1 (sunchoke, this-year's hard-harvest patch): plants are flowering. Yellow heads, tall stalks ~2m. Sunchoke flower means the plant has committed to seed and is now putting energy into tubers below ground. **This is the phenological moment Iri watches for**: from flower-onset, the tubers fatten over ~6-8 weeks. Her harvest window opens roughly Day 70-90 (late August through September).

Notebook: *"P1 D36 flower-onset. Tuber-fatten window D70-D90. Plan harvest D78-D85 (peak); current rotation says P1 is hard-harvest (1/3 of tubers) this year."*

Days 38-44 patch 11 (sorrel meadow): mid-summer competition surge. The meadow Iri keeps open is being pressured by woody seedlings she didn't catch in May — three more aspen suckers, two wild rose stems, a serviceberry sapling. She spends ~4 hours over 3 days cutting them at ground level. She also harvests sorrel leaves (about 200g fresh, eaten fresh and as a tart addition to the dried mushroom from P16) and notes that the perennial-onion sets she scattered at P11 last fall have taken in about 60% of the spots she planted — fewer than she hoped, but enough to call the scatter successful.

Notebook: *"P11 D38-44 — 6 woody seedlings cut. Sorrel ~200g harvested. P-onion scatter ~60% take rate. Re-scatter Day 80 once seed stocks ready."*

### Beat 10 — Day 45, the slip-and-fall (the second near-miss)

Day 45, Iri is descending the rocky ridge from patch 17 (balsam-fir stand) carrying a small bag of fresh resin she's collected for fire-starting + tea. Wet rock under leaf-litter on a slope she's walked dozens of times. **Her foot slips.** She catches herself on a sapling, goes down on one knee, scrapes her left palm on rock.

Damage assessment: knee bruise (no break), palm-scrape (~5cm × 2cm, surface; bleeds moderately), nothing structural. **At her age (late 30s) and in this terrain, a fall like this is a multi-week-of-bruise event, and could easily have been a broken ankle or a worse scrape.**

She returns to camp, cleans the palm with creek water, applies a plantain poultice from her own Botanist kit (Bo's Run 11 chain in micro), bandages with a strip of clean cloth from her tarp's spare-edge. This (fall, Day 45) is the basic exposure-to-terrain-hazard that long territorial routines produce — Iri walks her 5km territory most days; over 90 days that's potentially ~300km of terrain navigation; **a fall is statistically eventual.** Her own Botanist kit handled it.

**This is the central economic finding of the run.**

## Architecture Stress-Test results

### §1 No Dominant Strategy — VERDICT: HELD, and *more naturally* than farming holds it

Iri's run produced ~28,000 cal stored across 90 days at low daily labor (mean ~2.5 hr/day patch-tending + foraging, vs Ren's ~4-5 hr/day garden-plus-forage). She did not face wildlife defense (no bear, no deer fence — patches are not high-concentration-edibility-signals the way fenced gardens are), no drought-stress on crops (wild plants tolerate variance), no labor-debt pre-harvest (every visit returns something, even if small).

**Per-day caloric throughput**: Iri ~310 cal/day; Ren ~217 cal/day from garden (~13% of intake) + ~660 cal/day forage = ~877 cal/day total. Ren is higher-total because she's running forage AND garden. But Iri's *garden-equivalent* (her patch tending) is **higher-yield-per-tending-hour** than Ren's garden, and Iri's daily-rhythm slack lets her forage too (her patch visits ARE forage). The two arcs converge in total calories but differ sharply in labor profile and risk profile.

**The §1 question is not "who got more cal/day" — it's "do these two lanes occupy different positions in a viable tradeoff space"**. Answer:

- **Pure foraging** (no patch-tending): lowest risk, highest mobility, *lowest yield*, no return-pattern. Forager walks territory; takes what's there; moves on. ~400-700 cal/day caloric ceiling in this biome at peak season.
- **Wild-Tender (this run)**: low-medium risk (terrain exposure), moderate mobility (territorial circuit, ~5km), *moderate yield with multi-year compounding* (~300-400 cal/day average, with sunchoke spikes), strong return-pattern, **knowledge-asset accumulating**.
- **Cultivator-farming (Ren, Run 13)**: medium-high risk (wildlife defense, drought, cascade-vulnerability during clearing), low mobility (tied to garden), *high per-patch yield but high labor + high losses*. ~217 cal/day garden + parallel forage.
- **Builder-Earthworks-farming (Vasco, Run 14)**: high risk (flash flood, infrastructure cascade, water budget), low mobility, *high deferred yield if it works*, **zero yield in the run window**, infrastructure asset accumulates.

**Wild-Tender slots cleanly between pure forage and Cultivator-farming.** Lower yield ceiling than farming. Lower risk than farming. **More yield-per-labor-hour than farming for tended-patch crops**. Strong long-arc reward (multi-year compound). **This is a real lane.** ✅

### §2 Mechanical Behavior — VERDICT: HELD throughout

Every event arose from observable mechanical inputs. The ramp emerged when soil temperature + day-length hit threshold; Iri timed harvest to phenological state. The blackberry runners crossed from P12 to P1 because vegetative growth follows substrate moisture + sun. The mushroom log-cluster flushed because 3 years of cool-shaded oak-and-birch deadfall + Iri's spawn transplant met fruiting conditions. The snake basked under the log because warm sun on flat substrate is what snakes do. The wasp nest was where it was because yellowjackets nest in the ground in late summer.

**Nothing was scripted. Iri's run, like Ren's and Vasco's, is the system running on its own.** ✅

### §14.13 Saliency — VERDICT: NEW MODE PROPOSED (delta-mode / saliency-over-time)

Bo (Run 11) demonstrated **saliency on current entity state** (the hemlock's stem, the cougar's outline). Eira (Run 09) demonstrated **saliency on negative-selection** (the spiral grain, the fungal entry, the compression-wood lean — entities to *reject*). Iri demonstrates a **third mode**: **saliency on delta against player-recorded prior state**. The patch is the same patch; what differs is *what changed since last visit*. The notebook is the diegetic surface that makes this differential mode possible.

**Three saliency modes proposed for §14.13's catalog:**
1. **Positive-selection / state-read** — Bo's umbel, hunter's cougar, tracker's print (current entity properties)
2. **Negative-selection / rejection** — Eira's spiral grain, fungal entry, leaning yew (entities to refuse)
3. **Delta-read / over-time** — **Iri's patch density vs prior-year, runner-encroach vs prior-year, emergence-rate vs prior-year** (entity changes against player-recorded baseline)

**Mode 3 requires persistent world-state on the patches** (MW1) and **persistent player-recorded notes** (MW3) to mechanically support. **The notebook is therefore not optional; it is the diegetic surface for mode 3.** ✅ proposed

### Permadeath compatibility — VERDICT: COMPATIBLE, AND HARSH IN A WAY THAT FITS

If Iri had died on Day 45 from the fall (broken ankle 8km out from camp, slow starvation + exposure), the **4 years of patch knowledge** would have been on her corpse, in the notebook. **The next character — Iri's player's next character — would inherit either:**

- **The notebook, if it can be recovered.** This is the Field Notes (§14.12) analogue for Wild-Tender: cross-character knowledge inheritance via diegetic in-fiction artifact. Per §12.5, on solo/private the next character can pick up the recovered notebook. On public servers (server-isolated compendium), the notebook is a per-shard artifact — recoverable in that world, but the *previous character's player-knowledge* persists at the player level.
- **Nothing, if the notebook is destroyed** (rain on an unprotected notebook, fire, bear-ate-the-pack, river crossing accident). Then the patches revert — they still exist in the world, but the *knowledge of which is fallow, which is hard-harvest year, which individuals are marked for seed-save* is lost. A new character finds productive patches but can't replicate the rotation; over-harvest is the most-likely error. **4 years of work degrades over 1-2 seasons of misuse.**

**This is the right shape of harshness for the genre.** Death loses character; death-with-notebook-loss loses the *territorial asset*. **The patches themselves persist as world-state — they don't vanish with Iri's death.** Another character finding them sees productive wild plants. The knowledge of their structure is what dies with Iri.

**§14.12 Field Notes candidate for Wild-Tender deaths**: *"Iri walked her patches for four years. Her notebook recorded which sites had been hard-harvested in which year, which individuals were marked for seed-save, which patches were fallow. The notebook traveled in her pack. She died on a slick descent at the balsam-fir stand. Her pack went into the brush. Some characters who came after found the notebook; others found only patches they did not understand. The same plants. Different futures."* ✅

### Multiplayer interaction — VERDICT: ECONOMIC LANE OPENED

On PvP-aware public servers, Iri's marked patches are economic objects. Three multiplayer dynamics surfaced:

1. **Respect-the-marker culture** — Iri's red/yellow/blue/white ribbons are a §13.1 Forest-Signs-style vocabulary. A passing player who reads the ribbon vocabulary respects markers (red = don't take, yellow = ripe-for-harvest-go-ahead-modestly, white = fallow-don't-disturb). This is jungle-rules cooperation by signal.
2. **Theft / over-harvest** — another player can ignore the ribbons. The same 1/4 rule that Iri respects can be broken by a passing player who harvests 100% of a patch and moves on. Iri's tended patch crashes for 2-3 years. **The cost is borne by Iri's future runs, not the thief.** This is asymmetric cost — interesting design space; aligns with jungle-rules.
3. **Trade-on-knowledge** — Iri's notebook holds 4 years of patch locations + phenology. **Her knowledge is more valuable than her current harvest.** Two players who trade *map-shares* for *medicine* / *meat* are in a different exchange than two players trading material. Wild-Tender characters become **map specialists** in MP. ✅ proposed

Iri completed 90 days without firing a weapon (no bow, no slingshot, no rifle — just rock-throw opportunism for the one grouse). **The §15.6 constraint holds as it did in Run 11 for Bo.** Wild-Tender is a Forager-completable lane. ✅

## Design Inventory

### Wild-Tender techniques surfaced (recipe blocks)

**Patch competition cut (recipe block)**
- Inputs: knife or hatchet, identification of competition vs companion species
- Process: identify encroaching woody seedlings / invasive runners / over-population; cut at appropriate location (parent crown for runners, ground-level for woody seedlings); leave cuts as mulch
- Quality factor: cut-location read (runner-at-parent vs at-tip yields different re-send behavior); species-ID for companion-vs-competition (some plants help, some hurt)
- Cost: 30-90 min per patch per visit
- Output: patch maintained at productive density; competition delayed 1-2 seasons

**Patch marking (recipe block)**
- Inputs: cloth ribbon strips (colors per player code)
- Process: tie ribbon to plant or branch at eye-level; record code meaning in notebook
- Quality factor: ribbon vocabulary consistency; ribbon weather-resilience
- Cost: <1 min per marker
- Output: world-state object visible to player + (in MP) to other players; persists until weather-degraded or removed

**Seed-scatter (recipe block)**
- Inputs: saved seed, optionally cleared patch site
- Process: clear competing vegetation if needed; broadcast-scatter seed across cleared area; press lightly with trowel back; walk-firm once
- Quality factor: site selection (sun, drainage, prior-year scatter take-rate); seed quality from save discipline
- Cost: 10-30 min per scatter site
- Output: deferred yield (germination ~10-60% depending on species and site); fall-or-spring scatter depending on species

**Inoculation transplant (recipe block)**
- Inputs: spawn from productive natural source (donor log or root mass), recipient site (shaded deadfall for mushroom; cleared soil for plant)
- Process: cut small donor piece, transplant to recipient site, position in contact with substrate
- Quality factor: donor selection (mature productive source), recipient site selection (shade/moisture/substrate)
- Cost: 15-30 min per inoculation
- Output: 2-3 year deferred yield; once established, perpetual annual harvest

**Rotation discipline (recipe block, multi-year)**
- Inputs: notebook + multi-patch system (3-5 patches of same plant type)
- Process: hard-harvest one patch per year; fallow others; track in notebook; rotate annually
- Quality factor: notebook discipline; honest tracking
- Cost: nominal per-visit; long-arc record-keeping
- Output: sustainable multi-year yield; no patch-crash from over-harvest

### Notebook as item-with-data-value (proposed)

Iri's notebook is **the central kit item that holds player-knowledge as inventory**. Specification:

- **Form**: physical notebook + pencil; ~100 pages; pages can be damaged by water, fire, bear-attack-on-pack
- **Content**: patches (map + sketches), per-patch annual phenology dates, harvest yields per year, scatter sites + take rates, ribbon-code key, near-miss notes
- **§14.13 saliency dependency**: delta-mode saliency (proposed) reads from notebook content + world patch-state; without notebook, delta-mode collapses to current-state mode
- **Loss-mode**: water (wet notebook → smeared pages → partial data loss); fire (full destruction); theft/predator (full loss)
- **Recovery-mode**: a found notebook from a dead character carries forward the data — diegetic cross-character knowledge inheritance, similar to §14.12 Field Notes but at the level of **specific-territory-knowledge** not **general-mechanism-knowledge**
- **MP role**: the notebook is the highest-information-density object Iri carries; theft of a notebook costs more than theft of food
- **Compendium relation**: notebook is the *diegetic* surface for Wild-Tender-domain knowledge; the *compendium back-room* holds the generalized "Sunchoke" entry; notebook holds "this sunchoke field, my 4 patches, this rotation schedule"

### Patch-state-over-time as world-state (proposed)

For Wild-Tender to mechanically support delta-mode saliency, the following per-patch persistent world-state is required:

- **Density**: visible-individual-count or biomass estimate per patch
- **Last-harvest date + amount** (for rotation discipline)
- **Last-scatter date + take-rate** (for scatter-site calibration)
- **Competition load**: presence of invasive species, woody seedlings, runners from adjacent patches
- **Marker state**: ribbons present, their colors, their locations
- **Fallow status**: in-rotation, this-year hard-harvest, this-year fallow
- **Inoculation status** (mushroom logs especially): inoculated date, expected first-flush date

This is per-patch world-data, scoped per-shard. The state grid is much smaller than weather (§3.3) or scent (§12.8) — patches are countable per-territory (Iri has 18), not per-cell.

## EXPLORATORY DESIGN QUESTIONS

### Q1 — Is "Wild-Tender" a distinct Forager sub-node, or does it collapse into Forager-Expert?

**Answer: distinct sub-node, leaning strongly.** Six competence points (table in Architecture Stress-Test §14 above) do not appear in Forager-Expert / Botanist as scoped by Run 11. Patch-state-over-time saliency (MW2), runner-cut location (MW5), inoculation transplant (MW13), 1/3-and-1/4 rule discipline (MW10), seed-scatter site selection (MW6), and rotation discipline (MW8) are all stewardship-of-system competences distinct from individual-plant-Expert.

**The overlap with Forager-Expert is real**: plant-ID, harvest timing, look-alike discrimination are shared baseline. Wild-Tender is therefore proposed as a **Forager sub-node that builds on Botanist baseline** — Iri is Botanist-Expert AND Wild-Tender-Expert; Bo (Run 11) is Botanist-Expert and Wild-Tender-Novice; a generic Forager-novice is sub-baseline at both.

**Cost**: a new sub-node in §14's tree, with passive perks (debuff-removal on patch-state-read, on runner-discrimination, on seed-save-timing) and possibly signature abilities (e.g., "read 5-year phenology pattern from notebook + current patch state to forecast next year's yield"). Compendium domain extension for patch-system entries.

**Proposal**: Forager tree → Botanist sub-node OR Wild-Tender sub-node, with shared baseline. Player chooses sub-specialization at appropriate progression point. **This mirrors Run 09's Bowyer-as-Forester-sub-node finding and Run 14's Earthworks-as-Builder-sub-node finding.** Each primary node has 2-3 sub-nodes; the design has surfaced this pattern repeatedly.

### Q2 — Does managed-wild fill the gap between pure foraging and farming, or is it economically dominated by both?

**Answer: it fills the gap, with caveats.**

| Lane | Daily labor | Daily cal output (steady state) | Risk profile | Yield ceiling | Asset accumulation |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pure forage | 3-4 hr | 400-700 | Low (no territory commitment) | Low (~600 cal/day cap forest summer) | None |
| **Wild-Tender (this run)** | **2.5-3.5 hr** | **~300-700** (with sunchoke spikes pushing weekly average) | **Low-med (terrain exposure)** | **Medium (~28K cal/90 days demonstrated)** | **Notebook + patches + rotation knowledge** |
| Farming (Ren, Run 13) | 4.5-5.5 hr | ~217 garden + ~660 forage = ~877 | Medium-high (wildlife, drought, cascade) | Medium-high if losses controlled | Garden infrastructure + seed stock |
| Irrigation-farming (Vasco, Run 14) | 5-6 hr | ~0 in-window (deferred harvest) | High (flash flood, cascade) | High deferred | Infrastructure + perennials in long arc |

**Wild-Tender's economic niche**:
- **Higher yield-per-labor-hour than farming** (sunchoke 24K cal from 12 days of work, vs Ren's whole-garden 19.5K cal from 90 days)
- **Lower yield-ceiling than farming** (managed wild plants are not as dense per m² as a tended garden bed; you cannot make wild patches as productive per m² as cultivated, because the cultivars are inherently less productive than domesticates)
- **Lower risk than farming** (no wildlife fence-breach, no drought-induced crop-loss as severe, no cascade-vulnerability during clearing)
- **Higher cumulative-knowledge-asset than pure foraging** (4-year compound vs none)

**It is not dominated by either neighbor.** Pure foraging is mobile + low-commitment; Wild-Tender is territorial + multi-year-compounding. Farming is high-yield-ceiling + high-risk-and-labor; Wild-Tender is moderate-yield + low-risk-low-labor. **Three lanes, three positions.** ✅

### Q3 — Does Wild-Tender preserve §1 No Dominant Strategy *more naturally* than farming?

**Answer: yes, demonstrably.**

Run 13's §1-verdict for farming was "HELD, but only because the simulation produced losses" — i.e., wildlife + weather + labor cost all bit. If any one were softened, farming would dominate. **The §1 holding for farming is conditional on a sharp three-system load-bearing requirement.**

Wild-Tender's §1-verdict is "HELD by natural variance." The yield ceiling is **inherently lower** because wild cultivars are less productive than domesticates and patch density cannot be artificially boosted past natural sustainability without crashing the patch. **The lower ceiling is structural, not enforced by adversarial pressure.** Even an honest no-bear no-drought no-pest year would still produce ~30K cal/90 days, not ~100K — because that's what wild plants do.

**This is §1 preserved by the *botany* of wild plants, not by *adversarial systems*.** Iri's run had no bear, no flash flood, no drought, and the yield still landed inside the §1-acceptable range. **A softer simulation would not collapse Wild-Tender into dominance the way it would collapse farming.** ✅

### Q4 — Cross-character continuity: is the notebook worth a mechanic?

**Answer: yes, and it's an unusually clean fit for permadeath.**

Iri's notebook is the kind of artifact that **earns its loss-cost honestly**. 4 years of patch-knowledge IS the asset. Losing the notebook (water, fire, bear-on-pack) is a meaningful cost. **The harshness of multi-year-knowledge-loss is genre-correct for MN** — Long Dark, Zomboid, Rust all do permadeath that erases significant work. Wild-Tender's notebook-loss is in that tradition.

**Field Notes (§14.12) precedent**: account-bound mechanism-knowledge transfers across deaths. The notebook is the **territorial-knowledge** analogue: per-shard (because territory is per-shard), recoverable from corpse, destroyable. This is a clean parallel that adds depth without breaking existing structure.

**MP value**: the notebook on a public server is a coveted economic object. Theft of notebook ≠ theft of food. **This opens MP texture the design has not yet exploited.** ✅

### Q5 — Does Wild-Tender's multi-season character-state fit MN's permadeath model?

**Answer: yes, and it sharpens the permadeath stakes in a good way.**

Wild-Tender is the first surfaced design path that *rewards* multi-season character continuity not just at the §4.9 Year-as-Meta-Arc challenge level, but at the **cumulative-asset level**. A character who survives 2 years stewarding patches has more capability per visit than the same character in year 1 — not because of skill-level (skill caps at master per §14.8), but because of **accumulated patch-knowledge + accumulated patch-state**.

**Death cost is sharpened**: a Wild-Tender's death loses not just the character (§10.2) but the *implicit knowledge-of-territory* the character held in their head + their notebook. This is *more* than the §6 permadeath cost for Hunter/Builder/Survivalist — those characters' knowledge is mostly captured in compendium and field notes. **Wild-Tender's knowledge is in the player's actual memory and the notebook.**

**Is this too harsh?** Probably not. The compendium-back-room can capture *generalized* Wild-Tender knowledge ("sunchoke wants south-facing, 1/3 rule, 4-year rotation"). The *specific-territory-knowledge* (which patches are where, which are fallow this year, which individuals are seed-marked) lives in notebook + player memory. **Death-erases-the-specific is genre-appropriate.** ✅

### Q6 — Should MN's preferred design path be "Forager → Wild-Tender" rather than "Forager → Cultivator-farming"?

**Answer: this is the central scope question.**

**Pro Wild-Tender as the preferred fork**:
- Cleaner §1 preservation (yield ceiling is structural, not enforced)
- Smaller scope cost: ~6-8 new systems (patch-state, delta-saliency, notebook, scatter, rotation, inoculation, marker-vocabulary, multi-season continuity) vs farming's ~10-12 (soil, water, pest, disease, fence, cultivar-skill, etc.)
- Composes more naturally with existing world (no soil grid needed — patches sit in existing terrain; no separate water-demand layer — wild plants tolerate variance)
- More naturally permadeath-compatible (knowledge-asset cost)
- More mobile + territorial — fits the §1 nomadic-vs-settled balance better than fence-bound farming
- Opens richer MP texture (territorial markers, notebook trade, knowledge specialists)

**Pro farming as the preferred fork** (or as well as):
- Higher yield ceiling if all losses managed (Vasco's irrigation could feed a coop camp through winter)
- More familiar to genre players (Stardew, Valheim trained expectations)
- Builder/Mason/Earthworks have natural extension into farming infrastructure (cisterns, fences)
- Cooperative-scale dynamics richer in farming (shared garden vs shared notebook)

**Recommendation: lean Wild-Tender as the v1 fork**. Lower scope cost; better §1 preservation; cleaner permadeath fit. Farming can defer to a post-launch content arc analogous to §5.8 domesticated animals. **The "managed-wild" lane fills the gap that "farming" was the obvious-but-costly answer to.**

**If both are pursued**, Wild-Tender ships first, farming ships second on the foundation Wild-Tender lays (multi-season character continuity, patch-state world data — both used by Wild-Tender first).

## Big takeaways from Run 16

1. **Wild-Tender is a real Forager sub-node, not redundant with Botanist.** Six competence points (patch-state-delta read, competition-vs-companion read, seed-scatter site selection, rotation discipline, inoculation transplant, multi-season phenology tracking) are absent from Botanist-Expert as scoped by Run 11. The sub-node earns its keep.

2. **Managed-wild fills the gap between pure forage and farming.** Lower yield ceiling than farming (structural, not enforced); lower labor than farming; lower risk than farming; higher multi-year-compounding than pure forage. **Three lanes, three positions, preserves §1 cleanly.**

3. **§14.13 saliency has a third mode: delta-against-prior-state.** Positive-selection (Bo) + negative-selection (Eira) + delta-read (Iri). All three ride on the same shader system but query different data sources — current entity properties, entity-properties-to-reject, and entity-state-vs-recorded-baseline. **The notebook is the diegetic surface that makes mode 3 work.**

4. **Patch-state-over-time is new world-state data MN must add.** Per-patch density, last-harvest date, marker state, fallow status, inoculation status. Smaller than weather grid; per-territory, per-shard.

5. **The notebook is the most-load-bearing kit item this run surfaced.** It is the diegetic surface for Wild-Tender's signature saliency mode + the multi-year-knowledge asset + the MP-economic object. Its loss is meaningful in a permadeath-appropriate way.

6. **Permadeath sharpens cleanly around Wild-Tender.** Death loses the character (always); death-with-notebook-loss loses 4 years of territorial-knowledge. **This is genre-correct harshness.** The patches themselves persist as world-state; their *understanding* dies with the character.

7. **Wild-Tender is MN's preferred preferred-design fork over Cultivator-farming** (recommendation). Smaller scope cost (~6-8 systems vs ~10-12); cleaner §1 preservation; more natural composition with existing world; richer permadeath fit; opens novel MP texture.

8. **Three near-misses (snake Day 26, fall Day 45, wasp Day 88) are the honest risk surface** of a 90-day daily-territorial-routine. Wild-Tender is low-yield-low-risk-on-patches but compounds terrain-resident exposure over time. The risk shape is different from farming's wildlife/weather pressure, not absent.

9. **Iri lived, the patches persist, the notebook updates.** This is the run's honest outcome — not dramatic, not cinematic, just a forager continuing her relationship with the territory she's worked for 4 years. **The texture §1 + §2 promise: most days are not dramatic, and they don't need to be.** A character who returns to the same forest year after year, knows where the ramps come up, and watches her hop-pole tree gradually rot is playing the genre right.

10. **The decision on Wild-Tender is a *scope-decision*, not a design-discovery.** The architecture tolerates it cleanly. The systems required are bounded and compositional. The choice is whether MN commits to the lane (and recommends it as the design's preferred non-farming fork) or defers it (alongside farming, as a post-launch content arc). **The principles do not block it. The cost is moderate. The reward is a new player-archetype lane that fits Mother Nature's bones better than farming does.**

The recommended next step is the **scope-decision conversation**: commit to Wild-Tender as a v1 Forager sub-node (with patch-state + delta-saliency + notebook as the three load-bearing additions), commit to *both* Wild-Tender and Cultivator-farming sequenced (Wild-Tender first, farming on its foundation), or defer the whole question to post-launch (with §14 sub-node architecture laid as preparation regardless).
